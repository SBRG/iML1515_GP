CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transaldolase is important for the balance of
CC       metabolites in the pentose-phosphate pathway.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Sedoheptulose 7-phosphate + D-glyceraldehyde
CC       3-phosphate = D-erythrose 4-phosphate + D-fructose 6-phosphate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by Tris-HCl and phosphate buffer.
CC       Also competitively inhibited by sugars with L configuration at C2,
CC       such as D-arabinose-5-phosphate and L-glyceraldehyde.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=38 uM for D,L-glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate (with C3 acceptor
CC         compounds at pH 8.5 and 30 degrees Celsius)
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346};
CC         KM=90 uM for D-erythrose-4-phosphate (with C3 acceptor compounds
CC         at pH 8.5 and 30 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346};
CC         KM=31 mM for D-ribose-5-phosphate (with C3 acceptor compounds at
CC         pH 8.5 and 30 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346};
CC         KM=28 mM for D-glyceraldehyde (with C3 acceptor compounds at pH
CC         8.5 and 30 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346};
CC         KM=285 uM for D-sedoheptulose-7-phosphate (with C3 donor
CC         compounds at pH 8.5 and 30 degrees Celsius)
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346};
CC         KM=1200 uM for D-fructose-6-phosphate (with C3 donor compounds
CC         at pH 8.5 and 30 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346};
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is between 8.5 and 9.5. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346};
CC       Temperature dependence:
CC         Optimum temperature is between 15 and 40 degrees Celsius. At
CC         temperatures above 50 degrees Celsius, activity is lost rapidly,
CC         and at 55 degrees Celsius, the enzyme is totally inactivated.
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7592346};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Carbohydrate degradation; pentose phosphate pathway; D-
CC       glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate and beta-D-fructose 6-phosphate from D-
CC       ribose 5-phosphate and D-xylulose 5-phosphate (non-oxidative
CC       stage): step 2/3.
