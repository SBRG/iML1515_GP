CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pore-forming subunit of a potassium efflux system that
CC       confers protection against electrophiles. Catalyzes K(+)/H(+)
CC       antiport. Can also export rubidium, lithium and sodium.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01413, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17679694,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21041667, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9023177}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by glutathione. This inhibition is
CC       increased by NADH. Activated by adducts between glutathione and
CC       electrophiles. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17679694,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21041667, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9023177}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with the regulatory subunit KefF,
CC       forming a heterotetramer with 2:2 stoichiometry. Interaction with
CC       KefF is required for optimal activity. The active antiporter may
CC       be formed by the heterotetramer, or by an octamer.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01413, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17679694,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19523906, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21041667}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_01413, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2046548}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01413, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2046548}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The cytoplasmic RCK N-terminal domain regulates channel
CC       activity. It binds glutathione, resulting in inhibition of
CC       potassium efflux. In contrast, binding of the adducts formed
CC       between glutathione and electrophiles leads to activation of
CC       potassium efflux. Is expected to bind NADH, but X-ray
CC       crystallography shows bound AMP, and it would be difficult to
CC       accommodate NADH in this binding site (PubMed:21041667).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21041667}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the monovalent cation:proton antiporter 2
CC       (CPA2) transporter (TC 2.A.37) family. KefC subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01413}.
