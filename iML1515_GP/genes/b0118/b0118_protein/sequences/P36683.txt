CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the catabolism of short chain fatty acids
CC       (SCFA) via the tricarboxylic acid (TCA)(acetyl degradation route)
CC       and the 2-methylcitrate cycle I (propionate degradation route).
CC       Catalyzes the reversible isomerization of citrate to isocitrate
CC       via cis-aconitate. Also catalyzes the hydration of 2-methyl-cis-
CC       aconitate to yield (2R,3S)-2-methylisocitrate. The apo form of
CC       AcnB functions as a RNA-binding regulatory protein. During
CC       oxidative stress inactive AcnB apo-enzyme without iron sulfur
CC       clusters binds the acnB mRNA 3' UTRs (untranslated regions),
CC       stabilizes acnB mRNA and increases AcnB synthesis, thus mediating
CC       a post-transcriptional positive autoregulatory switch. AcnB also
CC       decreases the stability of the sodA transcript.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10585860, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10589714,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11932448, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12473114,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15882410, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8000525,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8932712}.
