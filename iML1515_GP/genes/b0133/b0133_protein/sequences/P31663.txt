CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the condensation of pantoate with beta-alanine
CC       in an ATP-dependent reaction via a pantoyl-adenylate intermediate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:357689}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + (R)-pantoate + beta-alanine = AMP +
CC       diphosphate + (R)-pantothenate.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Activation requires a combination of a divalent
CC       cation, magnesium or manganese, and a monovalent cation, potassium
CC       or ammonium. Above the optimum concentration for activation,
CC       magnesium and manganese are rather inhibitory. Also activated by
CC       2-mercaptoethanol, dithiothreitol, cysteine and glutathione.
CC       Inhibited by divalent cations (mercury, cobalt, zinc, copper,
CC       silver), chelating agents (EDTA, EGTA and o-phenanthroline), and
CC       analogs of beta-alanine (taurine, gamma-aminobutyrate, gamma-
CC       amino-beta-hydroxybutyrate). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:357689}.
