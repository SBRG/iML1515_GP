CC   -!- FUNCTION: Reversibly catalyzes the transfer of the carbamoyl group
CC       from carbamoyl phosphate (CP) to the N(epsilon) atom of ornithine
CC       (ORN) to produce L-citrulline, which is a substrate for
CC       argininosuccinate synthetase, the enzyme involved in the final
CC       step in arginine biosynthesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:789338}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Carbamoyl phosphate + L-ornithine = phosphate
CC       + L-citrulline. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01109}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=0.02 mM for carbamoyl phosphate {ECO:0000269|PubMed:789338};
CC         KM=5 mM for L-ornithine {ECO:0000269|PubMed:789338};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Amino-acid biosynthesis; L-arginine biosynthesis; L-
CC       arginine from L-ornithine and carbamoyl phosphate: step 1/3.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: In E.coli strain K12, trimer of identical or non-
CC       identical chains are composed of ArgI (I) and/or ArgF (F). The
CC       trimer has the following composition: FFI, FFF, FII, III. E.coli
CC       strains B and W, which are known to contain only ArgI, produce
CC       only a trimer of identical chains (III).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:4558857, ECO:0000269|PubMed:789338}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: ArgF might be the product of a relatively recent
CC       event (duplication or transposition) peculiar to E.coli strain K-
CC       12. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:4558857}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ATCase/OTCase family. {ECO:0000305}.
