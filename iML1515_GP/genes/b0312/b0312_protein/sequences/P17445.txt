CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the biosynthesis of the osmoprotectant
CC       glycine betaine. Catalyzes the reversible oxidation of betaine
CC       aldehyde to the corresponding acid. It is highly specific for
CC       betaine and has a significantly higher affinity for NAD than for
CC       NADP. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00804,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2194570, ECO:0000269|PubMed:3512525}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Betaine aldehyde + NAD(+) + H(2)O = betaine +
CC       NADH. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00804,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2194570, ECO:0000269|PubMed:3512525}.

CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Aldehydes with apolar groups are the most
CC       effective inhibitors. N-Methylated compounds are also inhibitory,
CC       but to a much lesser degree than the aldehydes. The alcohols are
CC       more inhibitory than the corresponding acids, and the inhibitory
CC       effect increases with the degree of methylation from ethanolamine
CC       to choline. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2194570}.
