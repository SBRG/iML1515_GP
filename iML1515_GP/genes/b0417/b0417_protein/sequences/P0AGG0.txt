CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the ATP-dependent phosphorylation of thiamine-
CC       monophosphate (TMP) to form thiamine-pyrophosphate (TPP), the
CC       active form of vitamin B1. Cannot use thiamine as substrate. Is
CC       highly specific for ATP as phosphate donor.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:4567662, ECO:0000269|PubMed:6284709}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + thiamine phosphate = ADP + thiamine
CC       diphosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:4567662}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Is markedly activated by the monovalent cations
CC       K(+), NH(4)(+), and Rb(+). Is significantly inhibited by ADP, AMP,
CC       p-chloromercuribenzoate, N-ethylmaleimide, pyrophosphate, and
CC       EDTA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:4567662}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=1.1 uM for thiamine-monophosphate
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:4567662};
CC         KM=270 uM for ATP {ECO:0000269|PubMed:4567662};
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is about 8.0. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:4567662};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cofactor biosynthesis; thiamine diphosphate biosynthesis;
CC       thiamine diphosphate from thiamine phosphate: step 1/1.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Reaction mechanism of ThiL seems to utilize a
CC       direct, inline transfer of the gamma-phosphate of ATP to TMP
CC       rather than a phosphorylated enzyme intermediate. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the thiamine-monophosphate kinase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
