CC   -!- FUNCTION: AcrA-AcrB-AcrZ-TolC is a drug efflux protein complex
CC       with broad substrate specificity that uses the proton motive force
CC       to export substrates. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16915237,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16946072, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17194213,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23010927}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in contact-dependent growth inhibition (CDI),
CC       acts downstream of BamA, the receptor for CDI. Its role in CDI is
CC       independent of the AcrA-AcrB-TolC efflux pump complex.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18761695}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotrimer, with large domains that extend into the
CC       periplasm, interacts with AcrA and TolC. AcrA may be required to
CC       stably link this protein and TolC. Interacts with AcrZ. Part of
CC       the AcrA-AcrB-AcrZ-TolC efflux pump. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10920254,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12374972, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12738864,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15228545, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16915237, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16946072,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17194213, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23010927}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Self; NbExp=4; IntAct=EBI-551006, EBI-551006;
CC       P0AAW9:acrZ; NbExp=4; IntAct=EBI-551006, EBI-6313593;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15228545, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15228545,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Positively regulated by MarA, Rob and SoxS
CC       transcriptional regulators (at protein level).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23010927}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Loss of susceptibility to contact-dependent
CC       growth inhibition (CDI); inhibiting cells still contact the
CC       target. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18761695}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the resistance-nodulation-cell division
CC       (RND) (TC 2.A.6) family. {ECO:0000305}.
