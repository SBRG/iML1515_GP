CC   -!- FUNCTION: AcrA-AcrB-AcrZ-TolC is a drug efflux protein complex
CC       with broad substrate specificity that uses the proton motive force
CC       to export substrates. This subunit may act as an adapter protein
CC       that links AcrB and TolC stably together. It is elongated in
CC       shape, being long enough to span the periplasm.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9878415}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomeric in solution. Homotrimeric; interacts
CC       indpendently with AcrB and TolC as well as AcrZ. Part of the AcrA-
CC       AcrB-TolC efflux pump. Complex assembly is independent of an
CC       efflux substrate and appears to be constitutive.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10920254, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15228545,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23010927, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9878415}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Self; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-875601, EBI-875601;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15228545, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137}; Lipid-
CC       anchor {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00303,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15228545, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137}. Note=An
CC       unlipidated version of this protein (directed to the periplasm by
CC       the OmpA signal sequence) functions normally.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cannot grow on efflux substrates novobiocin
CC       or fusidic acid. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9878415}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the membrane fusion protein (MFP)
CC       (TC 8.A.1) family. {ECO:0000305}.
