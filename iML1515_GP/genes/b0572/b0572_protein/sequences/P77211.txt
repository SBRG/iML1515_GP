CC   -!- FUNCTION: Forms pores that allow passive diffusion of cations
CC       across the outer membrane. Part of a cation efflux system that
CC       mediates resistance to copper and silver. In pathogenic strains it
CC       allows the bacteria to invade brain microvascular endothelial
CC       cells (BMEC) thus allowing it to cross the blood-brain barrier and
CC       cause neonatal meningitis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11399769,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12813074, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21249122}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotrimer. Component of the cus efflux system composed
CC       of CusA, CusB, CusC and CusF. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21249122}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell outer membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21249122}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21249122}. Cell outer membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21249122}; Lipid-anchor {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-
CC       ProRule:PRU00303, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21249122}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Transcriptionally regulated by CusR in response to
CC       copper and silver ions. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11283292}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The cus system plays an important role in copper
CC       tolerance under anaerobic growth and, under extreme copper stress,
CC       in aerobic growth.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the outer membrane factor (OMF) (TC 1.B.17)
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
