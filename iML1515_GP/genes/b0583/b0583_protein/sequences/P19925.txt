CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the biosynthesis of the siderophore
CC       enterobactin (enterochelin), which is a macrocyclic trimeric
CC       lactone of N-(2,3-dihydroxybenzoyl)-serine. The serine trilactone
CC       serves as a scaffolding for the three catechol functionalities
CC       that provide hexadentate coordination for the tightly ligated
CC       iron(2+) atoms. Plays an essential role in the assembly of the
CC       enterobactin by catalyzing the transfer of the 4'-
CC       phosphopantetheine (Ppant) moiety from coenzyme A to the apo-
CC       domains of both EntB (ArCP domain) and EntF (PCP domain) to yield
CC       their holo-forms which make them competent for the activation of
CC       2,3-dihydroxybenzoate (DHB) and L-serine, respectively.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8939709, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9214294,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9485415}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: CoA + apo-EntB/F = adenosine 3',5'-
