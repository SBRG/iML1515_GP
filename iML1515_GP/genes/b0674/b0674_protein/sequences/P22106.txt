CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the ATP-dependent conversion of aspartate into
CC       asparagine, using glutamine as a source of nitrogen. Can also use
CC       ammonia as the nitrogen source in vitro, albeit with lower
CC       efficiency. As nucleotide substrates, ATP and dATP are utilized at
CC       a similar rate in both the glutamine- and ammonia-dependent
CC       reactions, whereas GTP utilization is only 15% that of ATP, and
CC       CTP, UTP, ITP and XTP are very poor or not substrates. Also
CC       exhibits glutaminase activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20853825,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:6102982, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7907328}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + L-aspartate + L-glutamine + H(2)O = AMP
CC       + diphosphate + L-asparagine + L-glutamate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20853825, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7907328}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Glutamine-dependent asparagine synthesis
CC       activity can be inhibited by aspartic acid analogs (such as a
CC       sulfinate derivative and (2S,3R)-2-amino-3-methylsuccinate) in
CC       vitro; the inhibition is competitive with respect to aspartate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8691431}.
