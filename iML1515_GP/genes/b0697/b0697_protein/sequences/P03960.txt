CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of the high-affinity ATP-driven potassium transport
CC       (or Kdp) system, which catalyzes the hydrolysis of ATP coupled
CC       with the electrogenic transport of potassium into the cytoplasm
CC       (PubMed:2849541, PubMed:8499455, PubMed:23930894). This subunit is
CC       responsible for energy coupling to the transport system
CC       (PubMed:16354672). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16354672,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23930894, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2849541,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8499455}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + H(2)O + K(+)(Out) = ADP + phosphate +
CC       K(+)(In). {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00285,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2849541}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: ATPase activity is inhibited by vanadate,
CC       fluorescein isothiocyanate, N,N'-dicyclohexylcarbodiimide and N-
CC       ethylmaleimide. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2849541}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The system is composed of three essential subunits: KdpA,
CC       KdpB and KdpC (PubMed:2849541, PubMed:9858692). The complex also
CC       contains KdpF, a small non-essential subunit (PubMed:10608856).
CC       The KdpFABC complex exists as a dimer above concentrations of 30-
CC       50 nM, whereas the complex exists as a functional monomer at lower
CC       concentrations (PubMed:18298081). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10608856,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18298081, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2849541,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9858692}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_00285, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2849541}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00285, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Transcriptionally regulated by the KdpD/KdpE two-
CC       component regulatory system. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1532387}.
