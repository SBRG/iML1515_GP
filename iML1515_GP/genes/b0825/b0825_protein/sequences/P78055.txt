CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the reversible formation of fructose 6-
CC       phosphate from dihydroxyacetone (DHA) and D-glyceraldehyde 3-
CC       phosphate via an aldolization reaction. Can utilize several
CC       aldehydes as acceptor compounds in vitro, and hydroxyacetone (HA)
CC       or 1-hydroxy-butan-2-one as alternative donor substrate. Is also
CC       able to catalyze the direct stereoselective self-aldol addition of
CC       glycolaldehyde to furnish D-(-)-threose, and cross-aldol reactions
CC       of glycolaldehyde to other aldehyde acceptors. Is not able to
CC       cleave fructose, fructose 1-phosphate, glucose 6-phosphate,
CC       sedoheptulose 1,7-bisphosphate, xylulose 5-phosphate, ribulose 5-
CC       phosphate, and fructose 1,6-bisphosphate; cannot use
CC       dihydroxyacetone phosphate as donor compound nor D-glyceraldehyde
CC       as acceptor. Does not display transaldolase activity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11120740, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17985886,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19554584, ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: D-fructose 6-phosphate = glycerone + D-
CC       glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11120740,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17985886, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19554584,
CC       ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by glycerol, inorganic phosphate and
CC       arabinose 5-phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11120740}.

CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Is an interesting tool in chemoenzymatic synthesis
CC       for the construction of chiral complex polyhydroxylated sugar-type
CC       structures. The use of hydroxyacetone (acetol) as a donor compound
CC       allows access to various 1-deoxysugars. Can also be used for the
CC       synthesis of a broad range of iminocyclitols, that are potent
CC       glycosidase and glycosyltransferase inhibitors, from
CC       dihydroxyacetone, hydroxyacetone or 1-hydroxy-butan-2-one as donor
CC       substrate, and a range of acceptor substrates.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17985886, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19554584,
CC       ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Is not inhibited by EDTA which points to a metal-

CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the transaldolase family. Type 3A
