CC   -!- FUNCTION: Degrades proteins damaged by L-isoaspartyl residue
CC       formation (also known as beta-Asp residues). Degrades L-
CC       isoaspartyl-containing di- and maybe also tripeptides. Also has L-
CC       asparaginase activity, although this may not be its principal
CC       function. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11988085}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: May be involved in glutathione, and possibly other
CC       peptide, transport, although these results could also be due to
CC       polar effects of disruption. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11988085}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Cleavage of a beta-linked Asp residue from the
CC       N-terminus of a polypeptide.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=0.138 mM for beta-L-Asp-L-Leu {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15265041};
CC         KM=3.9 mM for L-Asn {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15265041};
CC         Note=No activity for GlcNAc-Asn, Gly-L-Asn, L-Asp, L-Asn-alpha-
CC         amide, L-Gln, aspartylglucosamides alpha- or gamma-aspartyl
CC         dipeptides.;
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotetramer of two alpha and two beta chains arranged
CC       as a dimer of alpha/beta heterodimers.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11053866, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11988085,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15159592, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15946951}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Repressed by cysteine, an effect that is attributed to
CC       CysB.
CC   -!- PTM: Autocleaved. Generates the alpha and beta subunits. The N-
CC       terminal residue of the beta subunit is thought to be responsible
CC       for the nucleophile hydrolase activity.
CC   -!- PTM: Both subunits undergo further processing at their C-termini.
CC       The overexpressed alpha subunit seems to consist of residues 2-
CC       161, with an oxidized Met residue and a tightly coordinated Na(+),
CC       whereas the overexpressed beta subunit is processed to residue 315
CC       and has 3 oxidized Met residues. Processing of the alpha subunit
CC       is inhibited by Zn(2+).
