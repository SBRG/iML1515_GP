CC   -!- FUNCTION: Channel that permits osmotically driven movement of
CC       water in both directions. It is involved in the osmoregulation and
CC       in the maintenance of cell turgor during volume expansion in
CC       rapidly growing cells. It mediates rapid entry or exit of water in
CC       response to abrupt changes in osmolarity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10400575, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10518952,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11493683}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10487750,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10518952, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10518953}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10487750, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10518952}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10487750,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10518952}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By extracellular hypoosmotic conditions, especially
CC       during the mid-logarithmic phase of growth.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Aquaporins contain two tandem repeats each containing
CC       three membrane-spanning domains and a pore-forming loop with the
CC       signature motif Asn-Pro-Ala (NPA).
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: It is a remarkably rigid tetramer that does not
CC       dissociate even when solubilized in SDS.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:10518952}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the MIP/aquaporin (TC 1.A.8) family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
