CC   -!- FUNCTION: Necessary for the introduction of cis unsaturation into
CC       fatty acids. Catalyzes the dehydration of (3R)-3-hydroxydecanoyl-
CC       ACP to E-(2)-decenoyl-ACP and then its isomerization to Z-(3)-
CC       decenoyl-ACP. Can catalyze the dehydratase reaction for beta-
CC       hydroxyacyl-ACPs with saturated chain lengths up to 16:0, being
CC       most active on intermediate chain length. Is inactive in the
CC       dehydration of long chain unsaturated beta-hydroxyacyl-ACP.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8910376}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: A (3R)-3-hydroxyacyl-[acyl-carrier protein] =
CC       a trans-2-enoyl-[acyl-carrier protein] + H(2)O.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8910376}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: (3R)-3-hydroxydecanoyl-[acyl-carrier-protein]
CC       = trans-dec-2-enoyl-[acyl-carrier-protein] + H(2)O.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8910376}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Trans-dec-2-enoyl-[acyl-carrier-protein] =
CC       cis-dec-3-enoyl-[acyl-carrier-protein].
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8910376}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Irreversibly inactivated by 3-decynoyl-N-
CC       acetylcysteamine (DNAC) which binds to the active site and forms
CC       an adduct (PubMed:21276098). {ECO:0000305|PubMed:21276098}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Lipid metabolism; fatty acid biosynthesis.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Mainly activated by FadR, but minor repression is also
CC       conferred by FabR (PubMed:11859088, PubMed:21276098).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11859088, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21276098}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the thioester dehydratase family. FabA
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
