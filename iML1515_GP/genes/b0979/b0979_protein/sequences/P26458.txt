CC   -!- FUNCTION: A terminal oxidase that catalyzes quinol-dependent,
CC       Na(+)-independent oxygen uptake. Prefers menadiol over other
CC       quinols although ubiquinol was not tested (PubMed:8626304).
CC       Generates a proton motive force using protons and electrons from
CC       opposite sides of the membrane to generate H(2)O, transferring 1
CC       proton/electron. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542282,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21987791, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22843529,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8626304}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 2 ubiquinol + O(2) + n H(+)(Side 1) = 2
CC       ubiquinone + 2 H(2)O + n H(+)(Side 2).
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=heme; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:30413; Evidence={ECO:0000305};
CC       Note=May bind up to 3 heme groups per complex. {ECO:0000305};
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by cyanide; is more sensitive to
CC       cyanide than cytochrome bd-I oxidase.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8626304}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Energy metabolism; oxidative phosphorylation.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterodimer of subunits I and II.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:8626304}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced when bacterial cultures reach stationary phase;
CC       synthesis is triggered by phosphate starvation or a shift from
CC       aerobic to anaerobic conditions. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1658595}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: 3-fold decreased ubiquinone levels but no
CC       change in redox levels of the ubiquinone pool (in aerobically
CC       grown minimal medium with glucose). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542282,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21987791, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22843529}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the cytochrome ubiquinol oxidase subunit 2
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Was originally thought not to translocate protons.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:19542282}.
