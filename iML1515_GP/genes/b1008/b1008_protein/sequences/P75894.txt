CC   -!- FUNCTION: May reduce toxic product malonic semialdehyde to 3-
CC       hydroxypropionic acid, which is excreted. RutE is apparently
CC       supplemented by YdfG. Required in vivo, but not in vitro in
CC       pyrimidine nitrogen degradation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16540542}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 3-hydroxypropanoate + NADP(+) = 3-
CC       oxopropanoate + NADPH.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=FMN; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:58210; Evidence={ECO:0000305};
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Up-regulated by the nitrogen regulatory protein C (NtrC
CC       also called GlnG) and repressed by RutR.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11121068, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17919280}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene lose the ability to
CC       utilize pyrimidine nucleosides and bases as the sole source of
CC       nitrogen at room temperature. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16540542,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20400551}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The Rut pathway degrades exogenous pyrimidines as
CC       the sole nitrogen source at room temperature but not at 37 degrees
CC       Celsius, a restriction that is apparently a consequence of an
CC       inadequate ability to remove toxic malonic semialdehyde at the
CC       higher temperature (RutE/YdfG function).
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the nitroreductase family. HadB/RutE
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
