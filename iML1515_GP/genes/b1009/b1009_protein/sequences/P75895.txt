CC   -!- FUNCTION: May increase the rate of spontaneous hydrolysis of
CC       aminoacrylate to malonic semialdehyde. Required to remove a toxic
CC       intermediate produce in vivo, but not in vitro in the pyrimidine
CC       nitrogen degradation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16540542}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: (Z)-3-aminoacrylate + H(2)O = malonate
CC       semialdehyde + NH(3).
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Up-regulated by the nitrogen regulatory protein C (NtrC
CC       also called GlnG) and repressed by RutR.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11121068, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17919280}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene form less than the
CC       normal amount of malonic semialdehyde because a portion of the 3-
CC       carbon intermediate is diverted out of the Rut pathway.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20400551}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The Rut pathway degrades exogenous pyrimidines as
CC       the sole nitrogen source at room temperature but not at 37 degrees
CC       Celsius, a restriction that is apparently a consequence of an
CC       inadequate ability to remove toxic malonic semialdehyde at the
CC       higher temperature (RutE/YdfG function).
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the AB hydrolase superfamily. Hydrolase
CC       RutD family. {ECO:0000305}.
