CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the pyrimidine ring opening between N-3 and C-
CC       4 by an unusual flavin hydroperoxide-catalyzed mechanism to yield
CC       ureidoacrylate peracid. It cleaves pyrmidine rings directly by
CC       adding oxygen atoms, making a toxic ureidoacrylate peracid product
CC       which can be spontaneously reduced to ureidoacrylate. Requires the
CC       flavin reductase RutF to regenerate FMN in vivo. RutF can be
CC       substituted by Fre in vitro. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16540542,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20400551}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Uracil + FMNH(2) + O(2) = (Z)-3-ureidoacrylate
CC       peracid + FMN + H(2)O. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20369853}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Thymine + FMNH(2) + O(2) = (Z)-2-
CC       methylureidoacrylate peracid + FMN + H(2)O.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20369853}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Up-regulated by the nitrogen regulatory protein C (NtrC
CC       also called GlnG) and repressed by RutR.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11121068, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17919280}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene lose the ability to
CC       utilize pyrimidine nucleosides and bases as the sole source of
CC       nitrogen at room temperature. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16540542}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The Rut pathway degrades exogenous pyrimidines as
CC       the sole nitrogen source at room temperature but not at 37 degrees
CC       Celsius, a restriction that is apparently a consequence of an
CC       inadequate ability to remove toxic malonic semialdehyde at the
CC       higher temperature (RutE/YdfG function).
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the NtaA/SnaA/SoxA(DszA) monooxygenase
CC       family. RutA subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
