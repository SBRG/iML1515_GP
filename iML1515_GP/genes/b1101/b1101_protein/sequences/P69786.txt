CC   -!- FUNCTION: The phosphoenolpyruvate-dependent sugar
CC       phosphotransferase system (sugar PTS), a major carbohydrate active
CC       transport system, catalyzes the phosphorylation of incoming sugar
CC       substrates concomitantly with their translocation across the cell
CC       membrane. The enzyme II complex composed of PtsG and Crr is
CC       involved in glucose transport (PubMed:3129430, PubMed:10562420).
CC       Also functions as a chemoreceptor monitoring the environment for
CC       changes in sugar concentration and an effector modulating the
CC       activity of the transcriptional repressor Mlc (PubMed:18319344).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10562420, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18319344,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:3129430}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: [Protein]-N(pi)-phospho-L-histidine + D-
CC       glucose(Side 1) = [protein]-L-histidine + D-glucose 6-
CC       phosphate(Side 2). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:3129430}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Transporter activity may be inhibited by SgrT.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16549791}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10562420,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12716891, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18319344,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:3023349}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-
CC       ProRule:PRU00426, ECO:0000305|PubMed:3129430}; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00426}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: The ptsG transcript is degraded under conditions of
CC       glucose-phosphate stress (due to intracellular accumulation of
CC       glucose-6-phosphate caused by disruption of glycolytic flux), or
CC       in the presence of (toxic) non-metabolizable glucose phosphate
CC       analogs due to hybridization with the small RNA sgrS (originally
CC       known as ryaA). This hybridization is sufficient to repress mRNA
CC       translation (PubMed:16549791). The hybrid is subsequently degraded
CC       by RNase E. This reduces the quantity of transporter and relieves
CC       glucose phosphate stress. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15522088,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16549791}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The EIIB domain is phosphorylated by phospho-EIIA on a
CC       cysteinyl or histidyl residue, depending on the transported sugar.
CC       Then, it transfers the phosphoryl group to the sugar substrate
CC       concomitantly with the sugar uptake processed by the EIIC domain.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00421}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The EIIC domain forms the PTS system translocation channel
CC       and contains the specific substrate-binding site.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00426}.
