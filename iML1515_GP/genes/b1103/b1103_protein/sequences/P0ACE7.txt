CC   -!- FUNCTION: Hydrolyzes purine nucleotide phosphoramidates, including
CC       adenosine 5'monophosphoramidate (AMP-NH2), adenosine
CC       5'monophosphomorpholidate (AMP-morpholidate), guanosine
CC       5'monophosphomorpholidate (GMP-morpholidate) and tryptamine
CC       5'guanosine monophosphate (TpGd) (PubMed:15703176,
CC       PubMed:20934431). Hydrolyzes lysyl-AMP (AMP-N-epsilon-(N-alpha-
CC       acetyl lysine methyl ester)) generated by lysine--tRNA ligase, and
CC       lysyl-GMP (GMP-N-epsilon-(N-alpha-acetyl lysine methyl ester))
CC       (PubMed:15703176). Is essential for the activity of the enzyme D-
CC       alanine dehydrogenase (DadA) and is required for E.coli to grow on
CC       D-alanine as a sole carbon source (PubMed:21754980). Is also
CC       required for growth at high salt concentrations (PubMed:15703176).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15703176, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20934431,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21754980}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15703176,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20934431}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene fail to grow on both
CC       D and L isomers of alanine due to a defect in DadA dehydrogenase
CC       activity, preventing D-alanine utilization (PubMed:21754980). The
CC       mutant cells do not have appreciable nucleoside phosphoramidase
CC       activity (PubMed:21754980, PubMed:15703176). The loss of hinT
CC       results in failure to grow in media containing 0.75 M KCl, 0.9 M
CC       NaCl, 0.5 M NaOAc, or 10 mM MnCl(2) (PubMed:15703176).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15703176, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21754980}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the HINT family. {ECO:0000305}.
