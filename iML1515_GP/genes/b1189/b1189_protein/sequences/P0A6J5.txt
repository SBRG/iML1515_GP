CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the oxidative deamination of D-amino acids.
CC       Has broad substrate specificity; is mostly active on D-alanine,
CC       and to a lesser extent, on several other D-amino acids such as D-
CC       methionine, D-serine and D-proline, but not on L-alanine.
CC       Participates in the utilization of L-alanine and D-alanine as the
CC       sole source of carbon, nitrogen and energy for growth. Is also
CC       able to oxidize D-amino acid analogs such as 3,4-dehydro-D-
CC       proline, D-2-aminobutyrate, D-norvaline, D-norleucine, cis-4-
CC       hydroxy-D-proline, and DL-ethionine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15358424}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: A D-amino acid + H(2)O + acceptor = a 2-oxo
CC       acid + NH(3) + reduced acceptor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15358424}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=FAD; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:57692; Evidence={ECO:0000250};
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Is activated by the HinT protein. Is inhibited
CC       by D-cycloserine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15358424,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21754980}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=30 mM for D-alanine {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:15358424};
CC         KM=6.4 mM for 3,4-dehydro-D-proline {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:15358424};
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is about 8.9 with D-alanine as substrate and about 9
CC         with 3,4-dehydro-D-proline as substrate.
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15358424};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Amino-acid degradation; D-alanine degradation; NH(3) and
CC       pyruvate from D-alanine: step 1/1.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15358424};
CC       Peripheral membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15358424}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By D-alanine. Is regulated by catabolite repression.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Loss of the ability to utilize both D- and
CC       L-stereoisomers of alanine as sole sources of carbon, nitrogen and
CC       energy for growth. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:13292}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the DadA oxidoreductase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Was originally thought to be a heterodimer based on the
CC       purification of the enzyme first reported from E.coli B, but
CC       results of enzyme assays in PubMed:21378189 have indicated that
CC       DadA is solely responsible for the observed dehydrogenase
CC       activity. {ECO:0000305}.
