CC   -!- FUNCTION: Protein deacetylase that removes acetyl groups on
CC       specific lysine residues in target proteins. Regulates
CC       transcription by catalyzing deacetylation of 'Lys-52' and 'Lys-62'
CC       of the transcriptional repressor RutR. Is also able to deacetylate
CC       NhoA and RluC in vitro. Targets a distinct set of substrates
CC       compared to CobB. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26716769}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the dihydroxyacetone kinase complex, which
CC       is responsible for the phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP)-dependent
CC       phosphorylation of dihydroxyacetone. DhaM serves as the phosphoryl
CC       donor. Is phosphorylated by phosphoenolpyruvate in an EI- and HPr-
CC       dependent reaction, and a phosphorelay system on histidine
CC       residues finally leads to phosphoryl transfer to DhaL and
CC       dihydroxyacetone. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11350937}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Note=Does not use NAD(+) or Zn(2+) like other established KDACs.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26716769};
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=2.13 uM for acetylated RutR {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26716769};
CC         Vmax=0.29 umol/min/mg enzyme for the deacetylation of RutR
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26716769};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:11350937). The dihydroxyacetone kinase
CC       complex is composed of a homodimer of DhaM, a homodimer of DhaK
CC       and the subunit DhaL. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11350937}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Consists of three domains. The N-terminal dimerization
CC       domain has the same fold as the IIA domain of the mannose
CC       transporter of the bacterial phosphoenolpyruvate:sugar
CC       phosphotransferase system (PTS). The middle domain is similar to
CC       HPr and the C-terminus is similar to the N-terminal domain of
CC       enzyme I (EI) of the PTS. The IIA domain of DhaM (via phospho-His-
CC       9), instead of ATP, is the phosphoryl donor to dihydroxyacetone
CC       (Dha). The phosphoryl flow likely involves HPr ('His-15') -> DhaM
CC       (His-430 -> His-169 -> His-9) -> DhaL-ADP -> Dha. The HPr-like
CC       domain of DhaM cannot efficiently substitute for the general
CC       carrier protein HPr. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11350937}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Unlike the carbohydrate-specific transporters of
CC       the PTS, the complex DhaKLM has no transport activity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11350937}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the PEP-utilizing enzyme family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
