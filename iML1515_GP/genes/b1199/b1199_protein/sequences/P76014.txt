CC   -!- FUNCTION: ADP-binding subunit of the dihydroxyacetone kinase,
CC       which is responsible for the phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP)-dependent
CC       phosphorylation of dihydroxyacetone. DhaL-ADP receives a
CC       phosphoryl group from DhaM and transmits it to dihydroxyacetone.
CC       DhaL-ADP acts also as a coactivator by binding to the sensor
CC       domain of DhaR. DhaL-ATP is inactive.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11350937}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Polyol metabolism; glycerol degradation.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The dihydroxyacetone kinase complex is composed of a
CC       homodimer of DhaM, a homodimer of DhaK and the subunit DhaL.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16647083}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The DhaL subunit corresponds to the C-terminal part of
CC       ATP-dependent dihydroxykinases and the DhaK subunit corresponds to
CC       the N-terminal part.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Unlike the carbohydrate-specific transporters of
CC       the PTS, the complex DhaKML has no transport activity.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Complexation with ADP increases the thermal
CC       unfolding temperature from 40 to 65 degrees Celsius. The tightly
CC       bound ADP participates in a double displacement phosphoryl
CC       transfer reaction and thus plays the same role as histidines,
CC       cysteines and aspartic acids in other phosphoprotein
CC       intermediates.
