CC   -!- FUNCTION: Dihydroxyacetone binding subunit of the dihydroxyacetone
CC       kinase, which is responsible for the phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP)-
CC       dependent phosphorylation of dihydroxyacetone. Binds covalently
CC       dihydroxyacetone in hemiaminal linkage. Acts also as a corepressor
CC       of DhaR by binding to its sensor domain, in the absence of
CC       dihydroxyacetone.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=6 uM for dihydroxyacetone {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15476397};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Polyol metabolism; glycerol degradation.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. The dihydroxyacetone kinase complex is
CC       composed of a homodimer of DhaM, a homodimer of DhaK and the
CC       subunit DhaL. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12813127,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15476397}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The DhaL subunit corresponds to the C-terminal part of
CC       ATP-dependent dihydroxykinases and the DhaK subunit corresponds to
CC       the N-terminal part.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Unlike the carbohydrate-specific transporters of
CC       the PTS, the complex DhaKML has no transport activity.
