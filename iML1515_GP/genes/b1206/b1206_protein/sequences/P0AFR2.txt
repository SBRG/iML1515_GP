CC   -!- FUNCTION: Responsible for the aerobic transport of succinate from
CC       the periplasm to the cytoplasm at acidic pH. Can transport other
CC       C4-dicarboxylic acids such as aspartate and fumarate. May also
CC       play a role in the regulation of C4-dicarboxylic acid metabolism
CC       at pH 7, via regulation of expression and/or activity of DctA. May
CC       act as a co-sensor of DcuS. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23278959}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23278959}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23278959}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Mutant is metabolically inactive when
CC       succinate is used as sole carbon source. Does not affect growth on
CC       glucose, fructose, maltose and glycerol.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23278959}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the SLC26A/SulP transporter (TC 2.A.53)
