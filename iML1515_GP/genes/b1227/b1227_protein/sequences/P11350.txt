CC   -!- FUNCTION: The nitrate reductase enzyme complex allows E.coli to
CC       use nitrate as an electron acceptor during anaerobic growth. The
CC       gamma chain is a membrane-embedded heme-iron unit resembling
CC       cytochrome b, which transfers electrons from quinones to the beta
CC       subunit.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Nitrite + acceptor = nitrate + reduced
CC       acceptor.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=heme; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:30413;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:12910261};
CC       Note=Binds 2 heme groups per subunit. Heme 1, called the proximal
CC       or heme Bp in PubMed:12910261, is located at the cytoplasmic
CC       interface, heme 2, called the distal or heme Bd, is located at the
CC       periplasmic interface. Electrons are transferred from the
CC       periplasmic to the cytoplasmic heme.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12910261};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Dimer of heterotrimers each composed of an alpha, a beta
CC       and a gamma chain. Alpha and beta are catalytic chains; gamma
CC       chains are involved in binding the enzyme complex to the
CC       cytoplasmic membrane. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12910261}.
