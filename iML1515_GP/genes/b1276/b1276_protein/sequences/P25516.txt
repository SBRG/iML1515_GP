CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the reversible isomerization of citrate to
CC       isocitrate via cis-aconitate. The apo form of AcnA functions as a
CC       RNA-binding regulatory protein which plays a role as a maintenance
CC       or survival enzyme during nutritional or oxidative stress. During
CC       oxidative stress inactive AcnA apo-enzyme without iron sulfur
CC       clusters binds the acnA mRNA 3' UTRs (untranslated regions),
CC       stabilizes acnA mRNA and increases AcnA synthesis, thus mediating
CC       a post-transcriptional positive autoregulatory switch. AcnA also
CC       enhances the stability of the sodA transcript.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10585860, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10589714,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11932448, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12486059,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1838390, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9421904}.

CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced upon entry into stationary phase by iron,
CC       oxidative and salt stress under aerobic conditions. AcnA is
CC       subject to CRP-mediated catabolite repression and ArcA-mediated
CC       anaerobic repression. AcnA is negatively regulated by ryhB RNA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11917098, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15672380,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17015655, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8000525,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9421904}.

CC       to peroxide stress. The acnAB double mutant does not grow on
CC       unsupplemented glucose minimal medium and does not respond under
CC       aerobic conditions to glutamate. The acnAB double mutant retains a
CC       low but significant aconitase activity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11932448, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12486059,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8000525, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9202458}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The AcnA activity over a broad pH range might be a
