CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the dephosphorylation of diacylglycerol
CC       diphosphate (DGPP) to phosphatidate (PA) and the subsequent
CC       dephosphorylation of PA to diacylglycerol (DAG). Also has
CC       undecaprenyl pyrophosphate phosphatase activity, required for the
CC       biosynthesis of the lipid carrier undecaprenyl phosphate. Can also
CC       use lysophosphatidic acid (LPA) and phosphatidylglycerophosphate
CC       as substrates. The pattern of activities varies according to
CC       subcellular location, PGP phosphatase activity is higher in the
CC       cytoplasmic membrane, whereas PA and LPA phosphatase activities
CC       are higher in the outer membrane. Activity is independent of a
CC       divalent cation ion and insensitive to inhibition by N-
CC       ethylmaleimide. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15778224,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18411271, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21148555,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8940025}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Phosphatidylglycerophosphate + H(2)O =
CC       phosphatidylglycerol + phosphate.

CC       1,2-diacyl-sn-glycerol 3-phosphate + phosphate.
