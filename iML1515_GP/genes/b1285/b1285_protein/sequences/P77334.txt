CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of a 2 protein system that seems to be dedicated to
CC       transcription of csgD. This protein decreases csgD transcription
CC       and thus decreases expression of adhesive curli fimbriae genes
CC       csgEFG, csgBAC/ymaD and adrA (yaic). Activity of this protein is
CC       antagonized by the diguanylate cyclase YdaM. In vitro has c-di-GMP
CC       phosphodiesterase activity. Cyclic-di-GMP is a second messenger
CC       which controls cell surface-associated traits in bacteria.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17010156}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Cyclic di-3',5'-guanylate + H(2)O = 5'-
CC       phosphoguanylyl(3'->5')guanosine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17010156}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P33358:mlrA; NbExp=2; IntAct=EBI-548149, EBI-1127668;
CC       P77302:ydaM; NbExp=5; IntAct=EBI-548149, EBI-544662;
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Constitutively expressed at both 28 and 37 degrees
CC       Celsius, during transition into stationary phase, more highly
CC       expressed on plates than in liquid medium. Expression is RpoS
CC       dependent and repressed by H-NS. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17010156,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19332833}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Leads to overproduction of curli fimbrae and
CC       autoaggregation in cultures grown at 28 degrees Celsius.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17010156}.
