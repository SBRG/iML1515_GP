CC   -!- FUNCTION: The phage shock protein (psp) operon (pspABCDE) may play
CC       a significant role in the competition for survival under nutrient-
CC       or energy-limited conditions. PspE catalyzes the sulfur-transfer
CC       reaction from thiosulfate to cyanide, to form sulfite and
CC       thiocyanate. Also able to use dithiol (dithiothreitol) as an
CC       alternate sulfur acceptor. Also possesses a very low
CC       mercaptopyruvate sulfurtransferase activity.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Thiosulfate + cyanide = sulfite + thiocyanate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11997041}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by thiosulfate above 100 mM,
CC       particularly at low cyanide concentrations (<5 mM). Inhibited by
CC       sodium sulfate or sodium chloride at 0.25 M which gives around 50%
CC       inhibition of rhodanese activity. Addition of sodium phosphate at
CC       the same concentration results in about 65% inhibition. Sulfite
CC       strongly inhibits PspE activity (1 mM sodium sulfite resulted in
CC       more than 50% inhibition of rhodanese activity).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11997041, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19088907}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=2.7 mM for thiosulfate (at pH 8.6 and at 25 degrees Celsius)
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11997041, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19088907};
CC         KM=10 mM for dithiothreitol (at pH 8.6 and at 25 degrees
CC         Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11997041,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:19088907};
CC         KM=32 mM for cyanide (at pH 8.6 and at 25 degrees Celsius)
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11997041, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19088907};
CC         Vmax=410 umol/min/mg enzyme toward cyanide (at pH 8.6 and at 25
CC         degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11997041,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:19088907};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18355042}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Periplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1712397}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By heat, ethanol, osmotic shock and infection by
CC       filamentous bacteriophages. Expression is positively regulated by
CC       cyclic AMP-cAMP receptor protein (cAMP-CRP). Expressed at higher
CC       levels during growth on glycerol, acetate or proline as carbon
CC       source relative to expression during growth on glucose.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11997041, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1712397,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19088907}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Catalysis proceeds by a classical ping-pong bi-bi
CC       reaction mechanism, whereby a covalent enzyme-sulfur intermediate
CC       is formed on the active site cysteine.
