CC   -!- FUNCTION: Essential for aminobenzoyl-glutamate utilization. It
CC       catalyzes the concentration-dependent uptake of p-aminobenzoyl-
CC       glutamate (PABA-GLU) into the cell and allows accumulation of
CC       PABA-GLU to a concentration enabling AbgAB to catalyze cleavage
CC       into p-aminobenzoate and glutamate. It seems also to increase the
CC       sensitivity to low levels of aminobenzoyl-glutamate. May actually
CC       serve physiologically as a transporter for some other molecule,
CC       perhaps a dipeptide, and that it transports p-aminobenzoyl-
CC       glutamate as a secondary activity. The physiological role of
CC       abgABT should be clarified. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17307853,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20190044}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Completely inhibited by 100 nM of sodium azide
CC       and slightly inhibited by 100 mM of potassium fluoride.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17307853}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Could be transcriptionally regulated by AbgR.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9829935}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene loss the p-
CC       aminobenzoyl-glutamate utilization phenotype.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9829935}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: It is suggested that in wild-type strain abgT would
CC       normally be cryptic or not expressed under conditions of growth on
CC       minimal medium. Altering the expression of abgT from its wild-type
CC       context appears to be the mechanism for obtaining the growth
CC       phenotype (PubMed:9829935). {ECO:0000305|PubMed:9829935}.
