CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the thiolytic cleavage of the beta-keto C8
CC       intermediate 3-oxo-5,6-dehydrosuberyl-CoA with CoA to yield the C6
CC       intermediate 2,3-dehydroadipyl-CoA and acetyl-CoA. Besides it
CC       catalyzes also the last step of the pathway, in which 3-oxoadipyl-
CC       CoA similarly is cleaved to acetyl-CoA and succinyl-CoA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17259607, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20660314,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9748275}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Succinyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA = CoA + 3-oxoadipyl-
CC       CoA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17259607, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20660314}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 2,3-didehydroadipyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA = CoA +
CC       3-oxo-5,6-didehydrosuberyl-CoA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17259607,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20660314}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Aromatic compound metabolism; phenylacetate degradation.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Activated by cAMP receptor protein (CRP), integration
CC       host factor (IHF) and by phenylacetyl-coenzyme A (PA-CoA) that
CC       prevents PaaX from binding its target sequences. Inhibited by
CC       PaaX. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10766858, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9748275}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Mutants are unable to use phenylacetate as a
CC       carbon source. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12846838}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the thiolase family. {ECO:0000305}.
