CC   -!- FUNCTION: N-acylates some free L-amino acids and their derivatives
CC       using a CoA thioester as cofactor. Using acetyl-CoA as a cofactor,
CC       substrate specificity is methionine sulfone > methionine
CC       sulfoximine > methionine sulfoxide > methionine. Asparagine,
CC       lysine, glutamine, aspartate and glutamate are very poor
CC       substrates. Using methionine as a substrate, cofactor preference
CC       is propionyl-CoA > acetyl-CoA >> butyryl-CoA. {ECO:0000269|Ref.4}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acetyl-CoA + L-methionine = CoA + N-acetyl-L-
CC       methionine.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Expression leads to increased levels of N-acylated
CC       L-amino acids which could be used in a number of applications.
CC       {ECO:0000269|Ref.4}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the acetyltransferase family. PAT/BAR
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
