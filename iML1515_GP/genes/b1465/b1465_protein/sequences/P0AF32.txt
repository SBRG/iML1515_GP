CC   -!- FUNCTION: This is a second nitrate reductase enzyme which can
CC       substitute for the NRA enzyme and allows E.coli to use nitrate as
CC       an electron acceptor during anaerobic growth. The gamma chain is a
CC       membrane-embedded heme-iron unit resembling cytochrome b, which
CC       transfers electrons from quinones to the beta subunit.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Nitrite + acceptor = nitrate + reduced
CC       acceptor.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=heme; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:30413; Evidence={ECO:0000250};
CC       Note=Binds 2 heme groups per subunit. Heme 1 is located at the
CC       cytoplasmic interface, heme 2 is located at the periplasmic
CC       interface. Electrons are transferred from the periplasmic to the
CC       cytoplasmic heme. {ECO:0000250};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Dimer of heterotrimers each composed of an alpha, a beta
CC       and a gamma chain. Alpha and beta are catalytic chains; gamma
CC       chains are involved in binding the enzyme complex to the
CC       cytoplasmic membrane.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein.
