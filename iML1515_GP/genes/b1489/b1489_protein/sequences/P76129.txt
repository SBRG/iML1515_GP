CC   -!- FUNCTION: Heme-based oxygen sensor protein displaying
CC       phosphodiesterase (PDE) activity toward c-di-GMP in response to
CC       oxygen availability. Involved in the modulation of intracellular
CC       c-di-GMP levels, in association with DosC which catalyzes the
CC       biosynthesis of c-di-GMP (diguanylate cyclase activity). Cyclic-
CC       di-GMP is a second messenger which controls cell surface-
CC       associated traits in bacteria. Has very poor PDE activity on cAMP
CC       (PubMed:15995192) but is not active with cGMP, bis(p-nitrophenyl)
CC       phosphate or p-nitrophenyl phosphate (PubMed:11970957). Via its
CC       PDE activity on c-di-GMP, DosP regulates biofilm formation through
CC       the repression of transcription of the csgBAC operon, which
CC       encodes curli structural subunits. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11970957,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15995192, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20553324}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Cyclic di-3',5'-guanylate + H(2)O = 5'-
CC       phosphoguanylyl(3'->5')guanosine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19764732}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=heme; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:30413;
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420;
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Has c-di-GMP PDE activity in both Fe(2+) and
CC       Fe(3+)-bound forms; this activity is increased 6-7 fold by binding
CC       of O(2) and CO (PubMed:15995192) and NO (PubMed:17535805). Has
CC       cAMP PDE activity only when the heme is in the Fe(2+) form. cAMP
CC       PDE activity is inhibited by oxidation of the heme iron and by
CC       binding of external ligands such as CO and NO. Also strongly
CC       inhibited by etazolate hydrochloride, a selective cAMP PDE
CC       inhibitor. PDE activity is inhibited in the absence of oxygen.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15995192, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17535805,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19764732}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=36 uM for c-di-GMP;
CC         Note=For the EAL domain, residues 532-799.
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15995192};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer (PubMed:19864414); has been previously
CC       suggested to be a homotetramer based on size exclusion
CC       chromatography (PubMed:11970957). Forms a complex with DosC.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11970957, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15005609,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19864414}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expressed in the late exponential growth phase and at
CC       higher levels in the stationary phase. A member of the dosCP
CC       operon. Expression is RpoS dependent and is higher at 28 degrees
CC       Celsius than at 37 degrees Celsius. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19332833,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20553324}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The EAL domain (residues 532 to 799) is a cyclic
CC       dinucleotide di-GMP (c-di-GMP) PDE hydrolyzing c-di-GMP to 5'pGpG;
CC       it has no activity on cAMP. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15995192}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Binding of an external ligand to the heme located in the
CC       N-terminal sensory domain displaces the distal heme ligand from
CC       Met-87 to the ligand and triggers a conformational change that
CC       regulates the activity of the C-terminal catalytic domain.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The heme-PAS domain (residues 1-139) forms homodimers.
CC   -!- PTM: The heme distal ligand is coordinated by Met-87 in the active
CC       Fe(2+) (ferrous) form, by O(2) in the O(2)-bound form and by H(2)O
CC       in the inactive Fe(3+) (ferric) form.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Binds O(2) with a dissociation constant of about 74
CC       uM.
