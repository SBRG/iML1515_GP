CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in glutamate-dependent acid resistance. Imports
CC       glutamate inside the cell while simultaneously exporting to the
CC       periplasm the GABA produced by GadA and GadB. The gad system helps
CC       to maintain a near-neutral intracellular pH when cells are exposed
CC       to extremely acidic conditions. The ability to survive transit
CC       through the acidic conditions of the stomach is essential for
CC       successful colonization of the mammalian host by commensal and
CC       pathogenic bacteria.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane; Multi-pass membrane
CC       protein.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By acidic conditions. Expression is regulated by a
CC       complex system involving RpoS, cAMP, CRP, EvgAS, H-NS, GadE, GadW
CC       and GadX. The level of involvement for each regulator varies
CC       depending upon the growth phase and the medium.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10383761, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11976288,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12399493, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12694615,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12867478, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12940989}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The glutamate analog L-trans-pyrrolidine-2,4-
CC       dicarboxylic acid (L-PDC) blocks the uptake of glutamate by
CC       selective inhibition.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the amino acid-polyamine-organocation (APC)
CC       superfamily. Glutamate:GABA antiporter (GGA) (TC 2.A.3.7) family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
