CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the phosphorylation of autoinducer-2 (AI-2) to
CC       phospho-AI-2, which subsequently inactivates the transcriptional
CC       regulator LsrR and leads to the transcription of the lsr operon.
CC       Phosphorylates the ring-open form of (S)-4,5-dihydroxypentane-2,3-
CC       dione (DPD), which is the precursor to all AI-2 signaling
CC       molecules, at the C5 position. Required for the regulation of the
CC       lsr operon and many other genes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15601708,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17557827, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20025244}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + (S)-4,5-dihydroxypentane-2,3-dione = ADP
CC       + (S)-4-hydroxy-2,3-pentanedione 5-phosphate. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_02053}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_02053}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the FGGY kinase family. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_02053}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Was originally thought to originate from human and was
CC       called BRAG1 (brain-related apoptosis gene 1) with a proposed role
CC       in apoptosis (PubMed:8649811). The DNA sequence of the region
CC       sequenced is more than 99% identical to that of this E.coli gene.
CC       Furthermore the claim of an 'extensive similarity to the Bcl-2
CC       family of genes' is not correct. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:8649811}.
