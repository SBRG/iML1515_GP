CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the NAD(+)-dependent oxidation of succinate
CC       semialdehyde to succinate. It acts preferentially with NAD as
CC       cosubstrate but can also use NADP. Prevents the toxic accumulation
CC       of succinate semialdehyde (SSA) and plays an important role when
CC       arginine and putrescine are used as the sole nitrogen or carbon
CC       sources. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17873044,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20639325, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7009588,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7011797}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Succinate semialdehyde + NAD(P)(+) + H(2)O =
CC       succinate + NAD(P)H. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17873044}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=13.3 uM for succinate semialdehyde (with 0.6 mM NAD, at pH 8
CC         and at 30 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7011797};
CC         KM=33.7 uM for succinate semialdehyde (with 0.1 mM NAD, at pH 8
CC         and at 30 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7011797};
CC         Note=The enzyme reduced NADP at 15 % of the rate of NAD
CC         reduction.;
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 8.2. Activity decreases sharply as the pH is
CC         raised above pH 9.2. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7011797};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Amino-acid degradation; 4-aminobutanoate degradation.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7011797}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By p-hydroxyphenylacetate, succinate semialdehyde (SSA)
CC       and putrescine. Highly expressed under several stress conditions
CC       together with many genes related to the metabolism of nitrogen
CC       compounds. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17873044,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20639325}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells are unable to grow on 4-
CC       hydroxyphenylacetate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:6756331}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the aldehyde dehydrogenase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
