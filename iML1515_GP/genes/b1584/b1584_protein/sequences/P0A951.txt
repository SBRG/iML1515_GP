CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the protection against polyamine toxicity by
CC       regulating their concentration (PubMed:7642535, PubMed:10986239).
CC       Catalyzes the transfer of an acetyl group from acetyl coenzyme A
CC       (AcCoA) to the primary amino groups of spermidine to yield N(1)-
CC       and N(8)-acetylspermidine (PubMed:8077207, PubMed:7052085,
CC       PubMed:6297970). It can also use polyamines such as spermine, but
CC       not putrescine (PubMed:7052085). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10986239,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:6297970, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7052085,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7642535, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8077207}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acetyl-CoA + an alkane-alpha,omega-diamine =
CC       CoA + an N-acetyldiamine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7052085,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8077207}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Heat shock, alkaline shift, ethanol treatment
CC       and poor nutrient availability produce increased concentrations of
CC       monoacetylated spermidine (PubMed:2061318). Inhibited by alkaline
CC       phosphatase, N(1),N(8)-bis(ethyl)spermidine (BESPD) and
CC       N(1),N(12)-bis(ethyl)spermidine (BESPM) (PubMed:8077207,
CC       PubMed:6297970). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2061318,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:6297970, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8077207}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=2 uM for AcCoA (in the presence of 3 mM spermidine at pH 7.5
CC         and 37 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8077207};
CC         KM=220 uM for spermine (in the presence of 10 uM AcCoA at pH 7.5
CC         and 37 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8077207};
CC         KM=1290 uM for spermidine (in the presence of 10 uM AcCoA at pH
CC         7.5 and 37 degrees Celsius) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8077207};
CC       Temperature dependence:
CC         The level of spermidine acetyltransferase activity increases at
CC         low temperature to prevent spermidine toxicity.
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10986239};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23908034,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8077207}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Constitutively expressed throughout log and plateau
CC       phases of growth. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:2061318}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene accumulate
CC       spermidine at late stationary phase and show a reduced cell
CC       viability due to a decrease in protein biosynthesis
CC       (PubMed:7642535). At 37 degrees Celsius, growth of mutant is
CC       normal in the presence of 0.5 or 1 mM spermidine. However,
CC       following a shift to 7 degrees Celsius, the addition of 0.5 or 1
CC       mM spermidine results in inhibition of cellular growth or cell
CC       lysis, respectively. Furthermore, at 7 degrees Celsius, spermidine
CC       accumulation resulted in a decrease in total protein synthesis
CC       accompanied by an increase in the synthesis of the major cold
CC       shock proteins CspA, CspB, and CspG (PubMed:10986239).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10986239, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7642535}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Acetylation neutralizes the charge of the
CC       polyamine, which is then typically excreted from the cell.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8077207}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the acetyltransferase family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
