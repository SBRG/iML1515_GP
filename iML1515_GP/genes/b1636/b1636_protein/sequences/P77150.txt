CC   -!- FUNCTION: Pyridoxal kinase involved in the salvage pathway of
CC       pyridoxal 5'-phosphate (PLP). Catalyzes the phosphorylation of
CC       pyridoxal to PLP in vivo, but shows very low activity compared to
CC       PdxK. Displays a low level of pyridoxine kinase activity when
CC       overexpressed, which is however not physiologically relevant.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15249053, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9537380}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + pyridoxal = ADP + pyridoxal 5'-
CC       phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15249053,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9537380}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01639};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cofactor metabolism; pyridoxal 5'-phosphate salvage;
CC       pyridoxal 5'-phosphate from pyridoxal: step 1/1.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9537380}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15547280}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene and cells lacking
CC       both pdxY and pdxK are not auxotrophs, meaning that the de novo
CC       pathway of PLP biosynthesis is functional. For PLP salvage, the
CC       pdxY single mutant can use both pyridoxine and pyridoxal, the pdxK
CC       single mutant can use pyridoxal but not pyridoxine, and the double
CC       mutant can no longer use both compounds.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9537380}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the pyridoxine kinase family. PdxY
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
