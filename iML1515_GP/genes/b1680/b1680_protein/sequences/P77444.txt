CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cysteine desulfurases mobilize the sulfur from L-
CC       cysteine to yield L-alanine, an essential step in sulfur
CC       metabolism for biosynthesis of a variety of sulfur-containing
CC       biomolecules. Component of the suf operon, which is activated and
CC       required under specific conditions such as oxidative stress and
CC       iron limitation. Acts as a potent selenocysteine lyase in vitro,
CC       that mobilizes selenium from L-selenocysteine. Selenocysteine
CC       lyase activity is however unsure in vivo.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10829016, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11997471,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12089140, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12876288,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12941942}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: L-cysteine + acceptor = L-alanine + S-
CC       sulfanyl-acceptor.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: L-selenocysteine + reduced acceptor = selenide
CC       + L-alanine + acceptor.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=pyridoxal 5'-phosphate; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:597326;
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Displays a strong preference for selenocysteine
CC       as a substrate in vitro and is only very sightly active using
CC       cysteine. The interactions with SufE and the SufBCD complex act
CC       synergistically to enhance, up to 50-fold, its cysteine
CC       desulfurase activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12876288,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12941942, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14644425}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cofactor biosynthesis; iron-sulfur cluster biosynthesis.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Interacts with SufE and the SufBCD complex
CC       composed of SufB, SufC and SufD. The interaction with SufE is
CC       required to mediate the direct transfer of the sulfur atom from
CC       the S-sulfanylcysteine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11827487,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12876288, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12941942,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14644425}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P76194:sufE; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-1124981, EBI-1124973;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Suf operon is under both the Fe-dependent Fur repressor
CC       and the oxidative stress dependent OxyR activator.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the class-V pyridoxal-phosphate-dependent
CC       aminotransferase family. Csd subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
