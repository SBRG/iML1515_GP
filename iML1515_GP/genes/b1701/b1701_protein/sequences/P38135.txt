CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the esterification, concomitant with
CC       transport, of exogenous fatty acids into metabolically active CoA
CC       thioesters for subsequent degradation or incorporation into
CC       phospholipids. It has a low activity on medium or long chain fatty
CC       acids and is maximally active on C6:0 and C8:0 substrates
CC       (PubMed:15213221). FadK is not expressed under aerobic growth
CC       conditions and it could functionally replace FadD under anaerobic
CC       conditions (PubMed:15213221). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15213221}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a short-chain carboxylic acid + CoA =
CC       AMP + diphosphate + an acyl-CoA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15213221}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 7.7 - 8.2. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15213221};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Membrane; Peripheral membrane protein.
CC       Note=Partially membrane-associated. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:15213221}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Expression independent of FadR (PubMed:12535077). FadK
CC       is not expressed under aerobic growth conditions, the levels of
CC       anaerobic expression vary with the terminal electron acceptor,
CC       with more expression during growth on fumarate than on nitrate (at
CC       protein level) (PubMed:15213221). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12535077,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15213221}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Probably starts on Met-1; overexpressed protein
CC       starting at Met-19 has lower activity, forms aggregates during
CC       reaction and is unstable in storage at -80 degrees Celsius
CC       (PubMed:15213221). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15213221}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ATP-dependent AMP-binding enzyme
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
