CC   -!- FUNCTION: ChbG is essential for growth on the acetylated
CC       chitooligosaccharides chitobiose and chitotriose but is
CC       dispensable for growth on cellobiose and chitosan dimer, the
CC       deacetylated form of chitobiose. Deacetylation of chitobiose-6-P
CC       and chitotriose-6-P is necessary for both the activation of the
CC       chb promoter by the regulatory protein ChbR and the hydrolysis of
CC       phosphorylated beta-glucosides by the phospho-beta-glucosidase
CC       ChbF. Catalyzes the removal of only one acetyl group from
CC       chitobiose-6-P to yield monoacetylchitobiose-6-P, the inducer of
CC       ChbR and the substrate of ChbF. It can also use chitobiose and
CC       chitotriose as substrates. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22797760}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: N,N'-diacetylchitobiose + H(2)O = N-acetyl-
CC       beta-D-glucosaminyl-(1->4)-D-glucosamine + acetate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22797760}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Diacetylchitobiose-6-phosphate + H(2)O = N-
CC       monoacetylchitobiose-6-phosphate + acetate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22797760}.
