CC   -!- FUNCTION: The phosphoenolpyruvate-dependent sugar
CC       phosphotransferase system (sugar PTS), a major carbohydrate active
CC       transport system, catalyzes the phosphorylation of incoming sugar
CC       substrates concomitantly with their translocation across the cell
CC       membrane. The enzyme II ChbABC PTS system is involved in the
CC       transport of the chitin disaccharide N,N'-diacetylchitobiose
CC       (GlcNAc2) (PubMed:10913118, PubMed:10913117). Also able to use
CC       N,N',N''-triacetyl chitotriose (GlcNAc3) (PubMed:10913117).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913117, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913118,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:2092358}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: [Protein]-N(pi)-phospho-L-histidine + N,N'-
CC       diacetylchitobiose(Side 1) = [protein]-L-histidine + N,N'-
CC       diacetylchitobiose 6'-phosphate(Side 2).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913117, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913118}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913118,
CC         ECO:0000305|PubMed:15654077};
CC       Note=Can also use copper and nickel with lower efficiency.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913118};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a complex with ChbB (EIIB) (PubMed:10913119,
CC       PubMed:10913122, PubMed:19959833). ChbA is a homotrimer
CC       (PubMed:10913122, PubMed:15654077, PubMed:19959833,
CC       PubMed:22593574). The interface of the homotrimer is stabilized by
CC       metal cations such as magnesium, copper or nickel
CC       (PubMed:15654077). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913119,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913122, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15654077,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19959833, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22593574,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:10913118}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By GlcNAc2, GlcNAc3 and beta-N,N'-diacetylchitobiose
CC       (Me-TCB). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10913117}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The PTS EIIA type-3 domain is phosphorylated by phospho-
CC       HPr on a histidyl residue. Then, it transfers the phosphoryl group
CC       to the PTS EIIB type-3 domain. {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-
CC       ProRule:PRU00418}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Was originally (PubMed:2179047) characterized as part of
CC       a cryptic cel operon for a cellobiose degradation system. The Cel+
CC       phenotype is due to mutations making expression chitobiose-
CC       independent and altering the substrate specificity.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:2179047}.
