CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the NAD(+)-dependent oxidative decarboxylation
CC       of D-malate into pyruvate. Is essential for aerobic growth on D-
CC       malate as the sole carbon source. But is not required for
CC       anaerobic D-malate utilization, although DmlA is expressed and
CC       active in those conditions. Appears to be not able to use L-
CC       tartrate as a substrate for dehydrogenation instead of D-malate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20233924}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: (R)-malate + NAD(+) = pyruvate + CO(2) + NADH.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17088549, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20233924}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420; Evidence={ECO:0000250};
CC       Name=Mn(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:29035; Evidence={ECO:0000250};
CC       Note=Binds 1 Mg(2+) or Mn(2+) ion per subunit. {ECO:0000250};
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: By the transcriptional regulator DmlR in the presence
CC       of D-malate or L- or meso-tartrate, under aerobic conditions. Is
CC       not induced by L-malate, D-tartrate or succinate. Is also induced
CC       at high levels under anaerobic conditions in the presence of D-
CC       malate and its expression is more than 5-fold greater than the one
CC       under aerobic conditions. Repressed by glucose or nitrate under
CC       anaerobic conditions. During aerobic growth, appears to be
CC       repressed by the DcuS-DcuR two-component system, and is not
CC       repressed by glucose. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20233924}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Deletion of dmlA inhibits aerobic growth on
CC       D-malate but does not impair slow anaerobic growth on D-malate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17088549, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20233924}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Aerobic growth on D-malate requires, in addition to
CC       DmlA, the presence of the aerobic C4-dicarboxylate transporter
CC       DctA, for D-malate uptake. Meso-tartrate or L-tartrate are not
CC       able to support aerobic growth. Slow anaerobic growth on D-malate
CC       is observed only when glycerol is also provided as an electron
CC       donor, and D-malate is used in fumarate respiration, so is not
CC       dependent on DmlA.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the isocitrate and isopropylmalate
CC       dehydrogenases family. {ECO:0000305}.
