CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the oxidation of glucose 6-phosphate to 6-
CC       phosphogluconolactone. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00966,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15113569, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17962566}.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Probable source of extracellular death factor (EDF,
CC       sequence Asn-Asn-Trp-Asn-Asn, NNWNN) following processing and
CC       amidation. This pentapeptide stimulates cell death mediated by
CC       MazF (PubMed:17962566). Artificial peptides with altered sequence
CC       show that NNGNN, GNWNG and NWN no longer stimulate MazF's
CC       endoribonuclease activity; other peptides (NNGN, GNWMM, NNWNG,
CC       NNNWNNN) retain MazF-stimulating activity. NNWNN, NNGN, GNWMM and
CC       NNWNG prevent cognate antitoxin MazE from inhibiting MazF;
CC       although NNNWNNN stimulates MazF it does not do so in the presence
CC       of MazE. EDF also stimulates ChpB's endoribonuclease activity in
CC       vitro; in this case NWN partially stimulates ChpB, whereas NNGNN,
CC       GNWNN, NNWNG, GNWNG and NNNWNNN do not. Only the wild-type EDF
CC       peptide prevents cognate antitoxin ChpS from inhibiting ChpB
CC       (PubMed:21419338). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17962566,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21419338}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: D-glucose 6-phosphate + NADP(+) = 6-phospho-D-
CC       glucono-1,5-lactone + NADPH. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00966}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Carbohydrate degradation; pentose phosphate pathway; D-
CC       ribulose 5-phosphate from D-glucose 6-phosphate (oxidative stage):
CC       step 1/3. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00966,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15113569}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: EDF binds to MazF; mutated EDF (sequence NNGNN) does not.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21419338}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Self; NbExp=2; IntAct=EBI-555656, EBI-555656;
CC   -!- PTM: Probably processed by the ClpPX protease to generate the
CC       extracellular death factor (EDF). It is thought that processing
CC       produces Asn-Asn-Trp-Asp-Asn which is amidated to generate Asn-
CC       Asn-Trp-Asn-Asn (Probable). {ECO:0000305|PubMed:17962566}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Loss of production of extracellular death
CC       factor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17962566}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase
