CC   -!- FUNCTION: A murein DD-endopeptidase with specificity for D-Ala-
CC       meso-diaminopimelic acid (mDAP) cross-links. Its role is probably
CC       to cleave D-Ala-mDAP cross-links to allow insertion of new glycans
CC       and thus cell wall expansion. Functionally redundant with MepM and
CC       MepH. Partially suppresses an mepS disruption mutant.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23062283}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Zn(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:29105;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:23062283};
CC       Name=Ca(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:29108;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:23062283};
CC       Note=Zinc, although calcium also allows some activity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23062283};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cell wall biogenesis; cell wall polysaccharide
CC       biosynthesis.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane {ECO:0000305}; Single-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000305}. Note=Uniform peripheral
CC       location, with partial enrichment at cell poles.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells are shorter in a single mutant, while
CC       triple envC-nlpD-mepM disruptions have defects in septation and
CC       cell separation and form long filaments (15-fold longer) and
CC       further yet by the quadruple disruption mutant (envC-nlpD-mepM-
CC       ygeR, over 21-fold longer). Quadruple mutants are less sensitive
CC       to ampicillin lysis (PubMed:19525345). A triple mepS-mepH-mepM
CC       mutant is inviable, whereas a double mepS-mepM will grow on a
CC       nutrient-poor medium but not on a rich medium, suggesting the 3
CC       endopeptidases are functionally redundant in vivo. Depletion
CC       experiments of the double or triple mutants lead to cell lysis, as
CC       well as significantly decreased incorporation of mDAP into
CC       peptidogylcan sacculi and increased amounts of the enzyme's
CC       substrate (Tetra-Tetra-anhydro muropeptide) (PubMed:23062283).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19525345, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23062283}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the peptidase M23B family. {ECO:0000305}.
