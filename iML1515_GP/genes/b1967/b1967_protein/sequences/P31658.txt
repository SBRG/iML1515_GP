CC   -!- FUNCTION: Protein deglycase that repairs methylglyoxal- and
CC       glyoxal-glycated proteins, and releases repaired proteins and
CC       lactate or glycolate, respectively. Deglycates cysteine, arginine
CC       and lysine residues in proteins, and thus reactivates these
CC       proteins by reversing glycation by glyoxals. Is able to repair
CC       glycated serum albumin, aspartate aminotransferase,
CC       glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase, and fructose biphosphate
CC       aldolase. Acts on early glycation intermediates (hemithioacetals
CC       and aminocarbinols), preventing the formation of Schiff bases and
CC       advanced glycation endproducts (AGE) (PubMed:26102038,
CC       PubMed:26774339). Has been reported to display chaperone,
CC       peptidase and glutathione-independent glyoxalase activities
CC       (PubMed:12235139, PubMed:12565879, PubMed:14731284,
CC       PubMed:15550391, PubMed:7848303, PubMed:21696459). However, these
CC       apparently disparate activities are all recruited to execute its
CC       protein deglycase primary function (PubMed:26102038,
CC       PubMed:26774339). Plays an important role in protecting cells from
CC       carbonyl stress, severe heat shock and starvation, as well as in
CC       acid resistance of stationary-phase cells (PubMed:12235139,
CC       PubMed:16796689, PubMed:17158627). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12235139,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12565879, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14731284,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15550391, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16796689,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17158627, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21696459,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26102038, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26774339,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7848303}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: (R)-lactate = methylglyoxal + H(2)O.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21696459, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7848303}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: An N(omega)-(1-hydroxy-2-oxopropyl)-[protein]-
CC       L-arginine + H(2)O = a [protein]-L-arginine + (R)-lactate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26102038, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26774339}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: An N(6)-(1-hydroxy-2-oxopropyl)-[protein]-L-
CC       lysine + H(2)O = a [protein]-L-lysine + (R)-lactate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26102038, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26774339}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: An S-(1-hydroxy-2-oxopropyl)-[protein]-L-
CC       cysteine + H(2)O = a [protein]-L-cysteine + (R)-lactate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26102038, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26774339}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: The glyoxalase activity is inhibited by copper
CC       and zinc cations, activated by ferrous cations, and inactivated by
CC       thiol-blocking reagents (PubMed:21696459, PubMed:7848303). The
CC       aminopeptidase activity is inhibited by iodoacetamide,
CC       dithiothreitol, EDTA and 1, 10-phenanthroline (PubMed:15550391).
CC       Binding of ATP at high temperatures induces a conformational
CC       change that reduces HchA surface hydrophobicity, interferes with
CC       its ability to capture substrate proteins and inhibits chaperone
CC       activity (PubMed:12235139). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12235139,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15550391, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21696459,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7848303}.

CC   -!- DOMAIN: Consists of a large A domain and a smaller P domain
CC       connected by a linker. The thermally induced motion of the
CC       flexible linker-loop region leads to the uncovering of a high-
CC       affinity substrate-binding site that is essential to capture
CC       nonnative proteins at high temperatures.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15173574}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene show a loss of
CC       glyoxalase and reduction in aminopeptidase activity
CC       (PubMed:15550391, PubMed:21696459). They accumulate methylglyoxal
CC       and are more susceptible to methylglyoxal than the parent strain
CC       (PubMed:21696459). Cells exhibit growth defects above 48 degrees
CC       Celsius and accumulate higher levels of peptides than wild-type
CC       (PubMed:14731284, PubMed:15550391). They display increased
CC       glycation levels (PubMed:26102038). Moreover, the double and
CC       triple mutants lacking yhbO and yajL, and yhbO, yajL and hchA,
CC       respectively, display impressive amounts of glycated proteins,
CC       suggesting that the YhbO, YajL and Hsp31 deglycases display
CC       relatively redundant functions (PubMed:26774339). The hchA mutant
CC       cells show decreased viability in methylglyoxal- or glucose-
CC       containing media (PubMed:26774339). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14731284,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15550391, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21696459,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:26102038, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26774339}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: According to some authors, HchA exhibits an
CC       exceedingly weak proteolytic activity against bovine serum albumin
CC       (BSA) and some peptidase activity against small single amino acids
CC       conjugated to a fluorogenic reporter (PubMed:12939276). Another
CC       report showed that HchA does not display any significant
CC       proteolytic activity but displays a physiologically relevant
CC       aminopeptidase activity (PubMed:15550391).
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:12939276, ECO:0000305|PubMed:15550391}.

CC       these results (PubMed:27903648 and PubMed:28013050).
