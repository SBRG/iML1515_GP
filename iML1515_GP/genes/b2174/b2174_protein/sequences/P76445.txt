CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the modification of the lipid A domain of
CC       lipopolysaccharides (LPS). Transfers a phosphate group from
CC       undecaprenyl pyrophosphate (C55-PP) to lipid A to form lipid A 1-
CC       diphosphate. Contributes to the recycling of undecaprenyl
CC       phosphate (C55-P) (PubMed:18047581). In vitro, has low
CC       undecaprenyl-diphosphate phosphatase activity (PubMed:17660416).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17660416, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18047581}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Ditrans-octacis-undecaprenyl diphosphate +
CC       alpha-D-Kdo-(2->4)-alpha-D-Kdo-(2->6)-lipid A = ditrans-octacis-
CC       undecaprenyl phosphate + alpha-D-Kdo-(2->4)-alpha-D-Kdo-(2->6)-
CC       lipid A 1-diphosphate. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01945,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18047581}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by BasR. This regulation does not
CC       occur at the level of transcription, but rather following the
CC       assembly of LpxT into the inner membrane.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20384697}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 7.0. Significant activity is observed from pH 5.5
CC         to pH 7.5. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18047581};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Bacterial outer membrane biogenesis; lipopolysaccharide
CC       biosynthesis. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01945,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18047581}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_01945, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17660416}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01945, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17660416}.
CC       Note=Transferase activity takes place on the periplamic side of
CC       the inner membrane. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01945,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18047581}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Deletion of the gene results in the
CC       production of LPS containing only the bis-phosphorylated lipid A
CC       species. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18047581}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: In E.coli, lipid A contains typically a
CC       monophosphate unit at positions 1 and 4' (bis-phosphorylated lipid
CC       A). However, one-third of the lipid A contains an unsubstituted
CC       diphosphate unit at position 1 (lipid A 1-diphosphate).
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:18047581}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the LpxT phosphotransferase family.
