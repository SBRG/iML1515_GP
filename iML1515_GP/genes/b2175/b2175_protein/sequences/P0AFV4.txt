CC   -!- FUNCTION: A murein DD-endopeptidase with specificity for D-Ala-
CC       meso-diaminopimelic acid (mDAP) cross-links. Its role is probably
CC       to cleave D-Ala-mDAP cross-links to allow insertion of new glycans
CC       and thus cell wall expansion. Functionally redundant with MepM and
CC       MepH. Also has weak LD-carboxypeptidase activity on L-mDAP-D-Ala
CC       peptide bonds. Partially suppresses a prc disruption mutant.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23062283, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9158724}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: GlcNAc-MurNAc-L-alanyl-gamma-D-glutamyl-meso-
CC       diaminopimelyl-D-alanine + H(2)O = GlcNAc-MurNAc-L-alanyl-gamma-D-
CC       glutamyl-meso-diaminopimelate + D-alanine.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cell wall biogenesis; cell wall polysaccharide
CC       biosynthesis.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell outer membrane {ECO:0000305}; Lipid-
CC       anchor {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00303}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Unable to grow on nutrient agar at 42
CC       degrees Celsius. A triple mepS-mepH-mepM mutant is inviable,
CC       whereas a double mepS-mepM will grow on a nutrient-poor medium but
CC       not on a rich medium, suggesting the 3 endopeptidases are
CC       functionally redundant in vivo. Depletion experiments of the
CC       double or triple mutants lead to cell lysis, as well as
CC       significantly decreased incorporation of mDAP into peptidogylcan
CC       sacculi and increased amounts of the enzyme's substrate (Tetra-
CC       Tetra-anhydro muropeptide). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23062283,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9158724}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the peptidase C40 family. {ECO:0000305}.
