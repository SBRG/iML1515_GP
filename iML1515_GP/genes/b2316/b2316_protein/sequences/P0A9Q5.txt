CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the acetyl coenzyme A carboxylase (ACC)
CC       complex. Biotin carboxylase (BC) catalyzes the carboxylation of
CC       biotin on its carrier protein (BCCP) and then the CO(2) group is
CC       transferred by the transcarboxylase to acetyl-CoA to form malonyl-
CC       CoA.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Controls translation of mRNA for both itself and the
CC       alpha-subunit (accA) by binding to a probable hairpin in the 5' of
CC       the mRNA. Binding to mRNA inhibits translation; this is partially
CC       relieved by acetyl-CoA. Increasing amounts of mRNA also inhibit
CC       enzyme activity.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + acetyl-CoA + HCO(3)(-) = ADP + phosphate
CC       + malonyl-CoA. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01395}.
