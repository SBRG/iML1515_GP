CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the attachment of glutamate to tRNA(Glu) in a
CC       two-step reaction: glutamate is first activated by ATP to form
CC       Glu-AMP and then transferred to the acceptor end of tRNA(Glu).
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Phosphorylation of GltX by HipA prevents it from being
CC       charged, leading to an increase in uncharged tRNA(Glu). This
CC       induces amino acid starvation and the stringent response via
CC       RelA/SpoT and increased ppGpp levels, which inhibits replication,
CC       transcription, translation and cell wall synthesis, reducing
CC       growth and leading to multidrug resistance and persistence.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + L-glutamate + tRNA(Glu) = AMP +
