CC   -!- FUNCTION: The purine nucleoside phosphorylases catalyze the
CC       phosphorolytic breakdown of the N-glycosidic bond in the beta-
CC       (deoxy)ribonucleoside molecules, with the formation of the
CC       corresponding free purine bases and pentose-1-phosphate. This
CC       protein can degrade all purine nucleosides including xanthosine,
CC       inosine and guanosine, but cannot cleave adenosine, deoxyadenosine
CC       or hypoxanthine arabinoside. Has a preference for the neutral over
CC       the monoanionic form of xanthosine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15808857,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:3042752, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7007808,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7007809}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Purine nucleoside + phosphate = purine +
CC       alpha-D-ribose 1-phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10400599,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15808857, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17151449,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:3042752, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7007809,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7559336}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Rapidly inactivated by p-
CC       chloromercuriphenylsulfonic acid (p-CMB). Dithiothreitol
CC       incubation restores the activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:3042752}.

CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 6.5-7.5 with guanosine as substrate. At pH higher
CC         than 7.5, there is a marked reduction in reaction rate and a
CC         steep drop at pH higher than 9. Below pH 6.5, there is a
CC         dramatic decrease in activity reaching virtually zero at pH 6.0.
CC         With xanthosine as substrate, the pH optimum is 5.8-7.2. In the
CC         reverse reaction with guanine or xanthine as substrates, the pH
CC         optimum is 6.5-8.0. The pH dependence of inosine cleavage does
CC         not vary between pH 6 and 8. Maximal activity with inosine as
CC         substrate is observed at pH 6.6. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15808857,
CC         ECO:0000269|PubMed:3042752};
CC       Temperature dependence:
