CC   -!- FUNCTION: In addition to its role in cysteine synthesis,
CC       stimulates the tRNase activity of CdiA-CT from E.coli strain 536 /
CC       UPEC; stimulation does not require O-acetylserine sulfhydrylase
CC       activity. CdiA is the toxic component of a toxin-immunity protein
CC       module, which functions as a cellular contact-dependent growth
CC       inhibition (CDI) system. CDI modules allow bacteria to communicate
CC       with and inhibit the growth of closely related neighboring
CC       bacteria in a contact-dependent fashion (experiments done in
CC       strains BW25113 and X90, both K12 derivatives). This protein is
CC       not required for CDI of strain EC93, whose toxin may function by
CC       forming inner cell membrane pores. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22333533}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: O-acetyl-L-serine + hydrogen sulfide = L-
CC       cysteine + acetate. {ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 3-chloro-L-alanine + thioglycolate = S-
CC       carboxymethyl-L-cysteine + chloride. {ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.

CC       serine. CysK forms a complex with CdiA-CT/CdiI (PubMed:22333533).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0A1E3, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22333533,
CC       ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.
