CC   -!- FUNCTION: Specifically catalyzes the cleavage of the D-lactyl
CC       ether substituent of MurNAc 6-phosphate, producing GlcNAc 6-
CC       phosphate and D-lactate. Is required for growth on MurNAc as the
CC       sole source of carbon and energy. Together with AnmK, is also
CC       required for the utilization of anhydro-N-acetylmuramic acid
CC       (anhMurNAc) either imported from the medium or derived from its
CC       own cell wall murein, and thus plays a role in cell wall
CC       recycling. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15983044,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16452451}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: (R)-lactate + N-acetyl-D-glucosamine 6-
CC       phosphate = N-acetylmuramate 6-phosphate + H(2)O.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15983044}.

CC         Note=kcat is 5.7 s(-1).;
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Amino-sugar metabolism; 1,6-anhydro-N-acetylmuramate
CC       degradation.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Amino-sugar metabolism; N-acetylmuramate degradation.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cell wall biogenesis; peptidoglycan recycling.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18837509}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced by MurNAc 6-phosphate that releases the
CC       repressor MurR from the DNA. Also up-regulated by the cAMP
CC       receptor protein crp via the binding of crp-cAMP to a class I site
CC       upstream of the murQ promoter. Repressed by MurR in the absence of
CC       MurNAc 6-phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18723630}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: A lyase-type mechanism (elimination/hydration) is
CC       suggested for the cleavage of the lactyl ether bond of MurNAc 6-
CC       phosphate, with the formation of an alpha,beta-unsaturated
CC       aldehyde intermediate with (E)-stereochemistry, followed by the
CC       syn addition of water to give product.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the GCKR-like family. MurNAc-6-P etherase
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
