CC   -!- FUNCTION: Cell wall formation. The enzyme has a penicillin-
CC       insensitive transglycosylase N-terminal domain (formation of
CC       linear glycan strands) and a transpeptidase C-terminal domain
CC       which may not be functional. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10542235}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: (GlcNAc-(1->4)-Mur2Ac(oyl-L-Ala-gamma-D-Glu-L-
CC       Lys-D-Ala-D-Ala))(n)-diphosphoundecaprenol + GlcNAc-(1->4)-
CC       Mur2Ac(oyl-L-Ala-gamma-D-Glu-L-Lys-D-Ala-D-Ala)-
CC       diphosphoundecaprenol = (GlcNAc-(1->4)-Mur2Ac(oyl-L-Ala-gamma-D-
CC       Glu-L-Lys-D-Ala-D-Ala))(n+1)-diphosphoundecaprenol + undecaprenyl
CC       diphosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10542235}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Transglycosylase activity can be inhibited by
CC       moenomycin.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cell wall biogenesis; peptidoglycan biosynthesis.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10542235}; Single-pass type II membrane
CC       protein {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Deletion results in an altered mode of
CC       murein synthesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10542235}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Due to the fact that PBP-1c can neither substitute
CC       for PBP-1a or PBP-1b, nor rescue a PBP-1a/PBP-1b double mutant, it
CC       is possible that PBP-1c has its own distinct function. Moreover it
CC       does not bind to most of the beta-lactams known to bind to other
CC       binding proteins, suggesting that the penicillin-binding domain
CC       must be different from those present in PBP-1a and PBP-1b. It may
CC       function as a transglycosylase only.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10542235}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: In the N-terminal section; belongs to the
CC       glycosyltransferase 51 family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: In the C-terminal section; belongs to the
CC       transpeptidase family. {ECO:0000305}.
