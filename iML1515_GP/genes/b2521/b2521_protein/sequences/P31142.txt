CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transfers a sulfur ion to cyanide or to other thiol
CC       compounds. Also has weak rhodanese activity (130-fold lower). Its
CC       participation in detoxification of cyanide may be small. May be
CC       involved in the enhancement of serine sensitivity.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 3-mercaptopyruvate + cyanide = pyruvate +
CC       thiocyanate.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14672665}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal region is the non-catalytic domain; the C-
CC       terminus contains the active-site cysteine residue and the CGSGVTA
CC       motif probably responsible for substrate specificity.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Contains two rhodanese domains with different primary
CC       structures but with near identical secondary structure
CC       conformations suggesting a common evolutionary origin. Only the C-
CC       terminal rhodanese domain contains the catalytic cysteine residue
CC       (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
