CC   -!- FUNCTION: A scaffold on which IscS assembles Fe-S clusters. Exists
CC       as 2 interconverting forms, a structured (S) and disordered (D)
CC       form. The D-state is the preferred substrate for IscS. Converts to
CC       the S-state when an Fe-S cluster is assembled, which helps it
CC       dissociate from IscS to transfer the Fe-S to an acceptor. It is
CC       likely that Fe-S cluster coordination is flexible as the role of
CC       this complex is to build and then hand off Fe-S clusters.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11577100, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22203963}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Carboxymethylation by iodoacetic acid blocks
CC       sulfur transfer to this protein. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11577100}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. Forms a heterotetramer with IscS; each subunit
CC       of the IscS dimer contacts an IscU monomer.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11577100, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22203963}.
