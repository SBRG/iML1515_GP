CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the removal of elemental sulfur and selenium
CC       atoms from L-cysteine, L-cystine, L-selenocysteine, and L-
CC       selenocystine to produce L-alanine, and transiently retains the
CC       released sulfur atom on a cysteine residue, in the form of a
CC       persulfide. Can also desulfinate L-cysteine sulfinate, which is
CC       the best substrate of the enzyme. Functions as a selenium delivery
CC       protein in the pathway for the biosynthesis of selenophosphate.
CC       Seems to participate in Fe/S biogenesis by recruiting the SufBCD-
CC       SufE proteins. Transfers sulfur to CsdE that increases the
CC       cysteine desulfurase activity of CsdA. Can also transfer sulfur
CC       directly to TcdA/CsdL in vitro. Appears to support the function of
CC       TcdA in the generation of cyclic threonylcarbamoyladenosine at
CC       position 37 (ct(6)A37) in tRNAs that read codons beginning with
CC       adenine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10829016,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15901727, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20054882,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23242255, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9278392}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: L-cysteine + acceptor = L-alanine + S-
CC       sulfanyl-acceptor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15901727,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9278392}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: L-selenocysteine + reduced acceptor = selenide
CC       + L-alanine + acceptor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15901727,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9278392}.
