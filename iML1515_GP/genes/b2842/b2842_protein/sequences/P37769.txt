CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the reversible reduction of 2,5-diketo-3-
CC       deoxygluconate (DKII or 4,6-dihydroxy-2,5-dioxohexanoate) into 2-
CC       keto-3-deoxygluconate (KDG or 2-dehydro-3-deoxygluconate) with a
CC       concomitant oxidation of NADH (Ref.4). To a lesser extent, can
CC       also reduce 5-keto-D-gluconate and oxidize D-gluconate and 1,2-
CC       propanediol (PubMed:24509771). Together with KduI, seems to play a
CC       role in the catabolism of hexuronates under osmotic stress
CC       conditions, substituting for the regular hexuronate degrading
CC       enzymes UxaABC and UxuAB whose expression is repressed in these
CC       conditions (PubMed:23437267). In vitro, also exhibits NADH-
CC       dependent 20-ketosteroid reductase activity against eukaryotic
CC       steroid hormone 11-deoxycorticosterone (11-DOC), which is
CC       converted into the product 4-pregnen-20,21-diol-3-one. In addition
CC       to 11-DOC, five other C21 steroid compounds (11-deoxycortisol,
CC       cortisol, corticosterone, cortisone, and 21-hydroxypregnenolone)
CC       are reduced by KduD, but steroids lacking the hydroxyl group at
CC       C21 position, such as pregnenolone, testosterone propionate,
CC       cortisone acetate, or progesterone, cannot be used as substrate
CC       (PubMed:24509771). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23437267,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24509771, ECO:0000269|Ref.4}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 2-dehydro-3-deoxy-D-gluconate + NAD(+) = (4S)-
CC       4,6-dihydroxy-2,5-dioxohexanoate + NADH. {ECO:0000269|Ref.4}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 4-pregnen-20,21-diol-3-one + NAD(+) = 11-
CC       deoxycorticosterone + NADH. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24509771}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:

CC         KM=0.230 mM for 11-deoxycorticosterone
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24509771};
CC         KM=0.190 mM for 11-deoxycortisol {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24509771};

CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24509771};

CC   -!- INDUCTION: Is under the control of KdgR repressor (Probable). Its
CC       expression is up-regulated in the presence of galacturonate and
CC       glucuronate (PubMed:23437267). Is also up-regulated in intestinal
CC       E.coli of mice fed a lactose-rich diet and down-regulated in
CC       E.coli of mice on a casein-rich diet (PubMed:22427493).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22427493, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23437267,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:15528647}.
CC   -!- BIOTECHNOLOGY: Could be used for the production of valuable
CC       bioactive 20-hydroxysteroids; these compounds have potential for
CC       pharmaceutical applications as inhibitors of steroid hormone
CC       metabolizing enzymes, for the treatment of breast cancer,
CC       endometriosis, and prostate diseases in humans.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:24509771}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the short-chain dehydrogenases/reductases
CC       (SDR) family. {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Most strains of E.coli do not exhibit 20-ketosteroid
CC       reductase activity against steroid substrates such as 11-DOC,
CC       despite containing a full-length kduD gene. This activity is
CC       observed in the K12 / DH5-alpha strain, whose disruption of the
CC       kdgR gene leads to the constitutive expression of KduD in this
CC       strain. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:24509771}.
