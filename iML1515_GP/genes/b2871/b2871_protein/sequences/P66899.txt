CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the alpha,beta-elimination reaction of both L-
CC       and D-alpha,beta-diaminopropionate (DAP) to form pyruvate and
CC       ammonia. In vitro the D-isomer of serine is degraded to pyruvate,
CC       though very poorly; other amino acids (L-serine, D- and L-
CC       threonine, D- and L-beta-Cl-alanine) are not substrates. In vivo
CC       allows poor growth on L-DAP or a DL-DAP mixture but not on D-DAP
CC       alone, this may be due to a poor promoter. DL-DAP is toxic in the
CC       absence of this enzyme, it may inhibit enzymes involved in the
CC       synthesis of pyruvate and aspartate, as well as amino acids
CC       derived from them. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12596860,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12821154, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22505717,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:22904288}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 2,3-diaminopropionate + H(2)O = pyruvate + 2
CC       NH(3). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12596860}.
