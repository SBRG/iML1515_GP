CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transfers a CMP moiety from CTP to Mo-molybdopterin (Mo-
CC       MPT) cofactor (Moco or molybdenum cofactor) to form Mo-
CC       molybdopterin cytosine dinucleotide (Mo-MCD) cofactor. Is specific
CC       for CTP; other nucleotides such as ATP and GTP cannot be utilized.
CC       Is also able to convert MPT to MCD in the absence of molybdate,
CC       however, with only one catalytic turnover.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542235}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: CTP + molybdenum cofactor = diphosphate +
CC       cytidylyl molybdenum cofactor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542235}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542235};
CC       Name=Mn(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:29035;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542235};
CC       Note=Mg(2+) is essential for activity. However, Mn(2+) is able to
CC       functionally replace Mg(2+) with a 50% reduced efficiency, whereas
CC       no MCD is produced with other divalent cations like Co(2+) or
CC       Ni(2+). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542235};
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=3.4 uM for CTP {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542235};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. Interacts with the Moco-binding chaperone PaoD.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542235, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21081498}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal domain determines nucleotide recognition
CC       and specific binding, while the C-terminal domain determines the
CC       specific binding to the target protein. When the N-terminal domain
CC       of MobA is fused to the C-terminal domain of MocA, comparable
CC       kinetic constants as wild-type MobA are obtained with GTP, and the
CC       activity with CTP is completely lost. Consistent results are
CC       obtained when the N-terminal domain of MocA is fused to the C-
CC       terminal domain of MobA: the kinetic constants with CTP are
CC       comparable with the ones found for wild-type MocA, although no
CC       activity with GTP is detected. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21081498}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: A disruption in the mocA gene impairs MCD
CC       biosynthesis in E.coli, resulting in an inactive PaoABC aldehyde
CC       oxidoreductase devoid of MCD cofactor.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19542235}.
