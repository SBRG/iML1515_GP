CC   -!- FUNCTION: Mechanosensitive channel that participates in the
CC       regulation of osmotic pressure changes within the cell, opening in
CC       response to stretch forces in the membrane lipid bilayer, without
CC       the need for other proteins. Forms an ion channel of 1.0
CC       nanosiemens conductance with a slight preference for anions. The
CC       channel is sensitive to voltage; as the membrane is depolarized,
CC       less tension is required to open the channel and vice versa. The
CC       channel is characterized by short bursts of activity that last for
CC       a few seconds.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: The channel pore is formed by TM3 and the loop between
CC       TM2 and TM3. After a sharp turn at Gly-113, an alpha-helix
CC       (residues 114-127) is oriented nearly parallel to the plane of the
CC       putative lipid bilayer. On the intracellular side of the channel,
CC       the permeation pathway of MscS does not connect directly to the
CC       cytoplasm but instead opens to a large chamber that is connected
CC       to the cytoplasm. This chamber resembles a molecular filter that
CC       could serve to prescreen large molecules before they are allowed
CC       passage to the transmembrane pore. The TM1 and TM2 helices appear
CC       to be likely candidates for mediating the tension and voltage
CC       sensitivities of MscS. Gating requires large rearrangements of at
CC       least the C-terminus.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homoheptamer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12446901,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12767977, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137}.

CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16079137}.
