CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the tagatose-1,6-bisphosphate aldolase
CC       KbaYZ that is required for full activity and stability of the Y
CC       subunit. Could have a chaperone-like function for the proper and
CC       stable folding of KbaY. When expressed alone, KbaZ does not show
CC       any aldolase activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11976750}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Carbohydrate metabolism; D-tagatose 6-phosphate
CC       degradation; D-glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate and glycerone phosphate
CC       from D-tagatose 6-phosphate: step 2/2.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Forms a complex with KbaY.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: In contrast to E.coli strains C and EC3132, K-12
CC       strains cannot grow on N-acetylgalactosamine and D-galactosamine,
CC       because they carry a deletion and thus lack active PTS systems
CC       specific for these compounds. Therefore, KbaYZ in K-12 strains is
CC       not involved in the degradation of these compounds.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the GatZ/KbaZ family. KbaZ subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CAUTION: Was originally thought to be a tagatose 6-phosphate
CC       kinase. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:8932697}.
