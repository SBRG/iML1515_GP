CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the biosynthesis of 3-deoxy-D-manno-
CC       octulosonate (KDO), a unique 8-carbon sugar component of
CC       lipopolysaccharides (LPSs). KdsD is not essential in the KDO
CC       biosynthesis and can be substituted by GutQ. Catalyzes the
CC       reversible aldol-ketol isomerization between D-ribulose 5-
CC       phosphate (Ru5P) and D-arabinose 5-phosphate (A5P).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12805358, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16199563,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16765569}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: D-arabinose 5-phosphate = D-ribulose 5-
CC       phosphate.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Completely inhibited by 10 uM of nickel,
CC       copper, cadmium and mercury ions. Inhibited by zinc with an IC(50)
CC       of 1-3 uM. Metal ion inhibition may be a mechanism to control
CC       activity in vivo. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12805358,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20039350}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=0.35 mM for Ru5P (at pH 8.5 and at 37 degrees Celsius)
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12805358};
CC         KM=0.61 mM for A5P (at pH 8.5 and at 37 degrees Celsius)
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12805358};
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 8.4. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12805358};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Carbohydrate biosynthesis; 3-deoxy-D-manno-octulosonate
CC       biosynthesis; 3-deoxy-D-manno-octulosonate from D-ribulose 5-
CC       phosphate: step 1/3.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Bacterial outer membrane biogenesis; lipopolysaccharide
CC       biosynthesis.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12805358,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20954237}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the SIS family. GutQ/KpsF subfamily.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
