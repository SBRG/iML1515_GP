CC   -!- FUNCTION: Iron-storage protein, whose ferroxidase center binds
CC       Fe(2+) ions, oxidizes them by dioxygen to Fe(3+), and participates
CC       in the subsequent Fe(3+) oxide mineral core formation within the
CC       central cavity of the protein complex. The mineralized iron core
CC       can contain as many as 2700 iron atoms/24-meric molecule.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10769150, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14636073}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: 4 Fe(2+) + 4 H(+) + O(2) = 4 Fe(3+) + 2 H(2)O.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10769150, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14636073,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19705876}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=heme b; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:60344;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:19391621};
CC       Note=Binds 1 heme b (iron(II)-protoporphyrin IX) group per dimer.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19391621};
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Fe cation; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:24875;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:19391621};
CC       Note=Binds 2 iron ions per subunit. The catalytic dinuclear iron-
CC       binding site within each subunit is known as the ferroxidase
CC       center. In BFR, the ferroxidase center appears to function as a
CC       true di-iron catalytic cofactor, rather than as a pore for the
CC       transfer of iron into the central cavity, as found for eukaryotic
CC       ferritins. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19391621};
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Iron oxidation is inhibited by Zn(2+), which
CC       binds at the ferroxidase center with a higher affinity that
CC       Fe(2+). The occupation of the ferroxidase center by Zn(2+) also
CC       severely restricts the ability of BFR to form an iron core.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10769150, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14636073,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19705876}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homooligomer of 24 subunits, arranged as 12 dimers, that
CC       are packed together to form an approximately spherical molecule
CC       with a central cavity, in which large amounts of iron can be
CC       deposited as a ferric-oxy-hydroxide mineral core.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17077480, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18946693,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19439409, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19705876,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21215826, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7664064,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:9867433}.
CC   -!- MASS SPECTROMETRY: Mass=18496; Mass_error=2; Method=Electrospray;
CC       Range=1-158; Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:7559480};
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: The internal surface iron site that binds iron 3 is
CC       important for the mineralization phase but not for Fe(2+) binding
CC       and oxidation at the ferroxidase center.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the bacterioferritin family. {ECO:0000305}.
