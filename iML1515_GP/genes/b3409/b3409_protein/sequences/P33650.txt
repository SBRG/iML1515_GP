CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transporter of a GTP-driven Fe(2+) uptake system,
CC       probably couples GTP-binding to channel opening and Fe(2+) uptake
CC       (PubMed:12446835, PubMed:19629046). A guanine nucleotide-binding
CC       protein (G proteins) in which the guanine nucleotide binding site
CC       alternates between an active, GTP-bound state and an inactive,
CC       GDP-bound state. This protein has fast intrinsic GDP release,
CC       mediated by the G5 loop (about residues 149-158). Presumably GTP
CC       hydrolysis leads to conformational changes and channel closing
CC       (PubMed:19629046). A GDP release mechanism involving a
CC       conformational change of the G5 loop (and thus the removal of the
CC       nucleotide-binding and stabilizing interactions) would
CC       significantly reduce the affinity for GDP, and conceivably be
CC       sufficient for catalysing nucleotide release (PubMed:25374115).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12446835, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19629046,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23104801, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25374115,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25517170, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8407793}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The isolated N-terminal domain (residues 1-274) forms
CC       trimers (PubMed:19629046, Ref.10,PubMed:25374115).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19629046, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25374115,
CC       ECO:0000269|Ref.10}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Self; NbExp=5; IntAct=EBI-7131497, EBI-7131497;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15919996}; Multi-pass membrane protein.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Iron uptake is repressed by the global regulator Fur in
CC       presence of Fe(2+) (PubMed:12446835). Induced by hydroxyurea
CC       (PubMed:20005847). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12446835,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20005847}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: Contains a guanine-nucleotide-specific nucleotide-binding
CC       site (about residues 1-276) that shows slow GTP hydrolysis
CC       (PubMed:12446835, PubMed:23104801). When a non-hydrolyzable GTP
CC       analog binds to the trimeric N-terminal domain, it induces a
CC       structural shift which opens a pore (PubMed:19629046).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12446835, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19629046,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:23104801}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Poor uptake of Fe(2+).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8407793}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TRAFAC class TrmE-Era-EngA-EngB-Septin-
CC       like GTPase superfamily. FeoB GTPase (TC 9.A.8) family.
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU01048}.
