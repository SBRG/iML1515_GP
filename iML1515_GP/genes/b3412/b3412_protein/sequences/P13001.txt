CC   -!- FUNCTION: The physiological role of BioH is to remove the methyl
CC       group introduced by BioC when the pimeloyl moiety is complete. It
CC       allows to synthesize pimeloyl-ACP via the fatty acid synthetic
CC       pathway through the hydrolysis of the ester bonds of pimeloyl-ACP
CC       esters. E.coli employs a methylation and demethylation strategy to
CC       allow elongation of a temporarily disguised malonate moiety to a
CC       pimelate moiety by the fatty acid synthetic enzymes. BioH shows a
CC       preference for short chain fatty acid esters (acyl chain length of
CC       up to 6 carbons) and short chain p-nitrophenyl esters. Also
CC       displays a weak thioesterase activity. Can form a complex with
CC       CoA, and may be involved in the condensation of CoA and pimelic
CC       acid into pimeloyl-CoA, a precursor in biotin biosynthesis.
CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the hydrolysis of the methyl ester bond of
CC       dimethylbutyryl-S-methyl mercaptopropionate (DMB-S-MMP) to yield
CC       dimethylbutyryl mercaptopropionic acid (DMBS-MPA) during the
CC       biocatalytic conversion of simvastin acid from monacolin J acid.
CC       Can also use acyl carriers such as dimethylbutyryl-S-ethyl
CC       mercaptopropionate (DMB-S-EMP) and dimethylbutyryl-S-methyl
CC       thioglycolate (DMB-S-MTG) as the thioester substrates.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Pimeloyl-[acyl-carrier protein] methyl ester +
CC       H(2)O = pimeloyl-[acyl-carrier protein] + methanol.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11904168}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Strongly inhibited by phenylmethylsulfonyl
CC       fluoride (PMSF).
