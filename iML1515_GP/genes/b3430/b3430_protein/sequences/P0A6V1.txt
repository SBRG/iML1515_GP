CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the biosynthesis of ADP-glucose, a building
CC       block required for the elongation reactions to produce glycogen.
CC       Catalyzes the reaction between ATP and alpha-D-glucose 1-phosphate
CC       (G1P) to produce pyrophosphate and ADP-Glc.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648099}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + alpha-D-glucose 1-phosphate =
CC       diphosphate + ADP-glucose. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648099}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Allosterically activated by fructose-1,6-
CC       bisphosphate (F16BP), hexanediol 1,6-bisphosphate, NADPH and
CC       pyridoxal phosphate. Inhibited by AMP and by high concentrations
CC       of MgCl(2). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648099,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2162151, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21741429}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=25 uM for alpha-D-glucose 1-phosphate
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648099};
CC         KM=120 uM for diphosphate {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648099};
CC         Vmax=133 umol/min/mg enzyme toward alpha-D-glucose 1-phosphate
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648099};
CC         Vmax=97 umol/min/mg enzyme toward diphosphate
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648099};
CC         Note=Kcat is 111 sec(-1) for adenylyltransferase activity. Kcat
CC         is 81 sec(-1) for pyrophosphorylase activity.
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648099};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Glycan biosynthesis; glycogen biosynthesis.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homotetramer; dimer of dimers.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27545622, ECO:0000269|PubMed:826540}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Displays cooperative behavior and a bibi mechanism
CC       with sequential binding of ATP and G1P, followed by ordered
CC       release of pyrophosphate and ADP-Glc.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:27545622}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the bacterial/plant glucose-1-phosphate
CC       adenylyltransferase family. {ECO:0000305}.
