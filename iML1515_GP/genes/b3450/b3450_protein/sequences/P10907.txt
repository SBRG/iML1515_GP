CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of the ABC transporter complex UgpABCE involved in
CC       sn-glycerol-3-phosphate import. Responsible for energy coupling to
CC       the transport system (Probable). Can also transport
CC       glycerophosphoryl diesters. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01727,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2842304, ECO:0000269|PubMed:363686,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7042685, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8282692,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8407831, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + H(2)O + glycerol-3-phosphate(Out) = ADP
CC       + phosphate + glycerol-3-phosphate(In). {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-
CC       Rule:MF_01727}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Activated by gluconate, inhibited by fumarate
CC       and internal phosphate. Internal phosphate may bind to UgpC and
CC       reduce its affinity for UgpA and UgpE.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:363686}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The complex is composed of two ATP-binding proteins
CC       (UgpC), two transmembrane proteins (UgpA and UgpE) and a solute-
CC       binding protein (UgpB). {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01727}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane; Peripheral membrane
CC       protein.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced by phosphate starvation, via PhoB. Also induced
CC       by carbon starvation, via the cAMP receptor protein (CRP).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1987150, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2842304,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:7042685}.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: Functional exchangeability of MalK and UgpC. But
CC       UgpC does not complement the regulatory function of MalK.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the ABC transporter superfamily. sn-
CC       glycerol-3-phosphate importer (TC 3.A.1.1.3) family.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01727}.
