CC   -!- FUNCTION: A bis-(3'-5') cyclic diguanylic acid (c-di-GMP)
CC       phosphodiesterase involved in regulating the levels of c-di-GMP
CC       that control cell motility (the flagella) and adhesion (adhesive
CC       curli fimbriae). The YhjH effect on flagella is controlled via the
CC       c-di-GMP-binding flagellar brake protein YcgR, however curli
CC       expression is not regulated via YcgR. Forms a network with
CC       different diguanylate cyclases (DosC, YeaJ, YedQ, YegE have been
CC       identified) to regulate c-di-GMP levels. Flagellar activity is
CC       high at low c-di-GMP levels whereas curli fimbria are induced at
CC       high c-di-GMP levels. Cyclic-di-GMP is a second messenger which
CC       controls cell surface-associated traits in bacteria.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11031114}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Cyclic di-3',5'-guanylate + H(2)O = 5'-
CC       phosphoguanylyl(3'->5')guanosine. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18765794}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: A class 3 flagellar gene, it is dependent on FliA for
CC       expression. Induced in early post-exponential phase at both 28 and
CC       37 degrees Celsius, it shuts down later than class 1 and class 2
CC       operon genes. Expression has to be shut down for adhesive curli
CC       fimbriae to be induced, i.e. on solid medium where biofilms form.
CC       Repressed by RpoS. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18765794,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19332833}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Disruption leads to increased expression of
CC       adhesive curli fimbriae genes (PubMed:18765794), including CsgD
CC       (PubMed:19332833). Also leads to a severe reduction in swarm size
CC       and swimming velocity, and 80% reduced concentrations of c-di-GMP.
CC       Disruption of ycgR, or concomitant disruption of dosC, yegE, yedQ
CC       and yfiN completely restores motility, suggesting these 4 genes,
CC       together with this c-di-GMP phosphodiesterase, form a network that
CC       regulates cell motility by altering levels of c-di-GMP
CC       (PubMed:20303158). Overlapping but different results were seen by
CC       another group, where disruption of yeaJ, another probable
CC       diguanylate cyclase, partially suppresses the yhjH disruption,
CC       full suppression requires concomitant disruption of yeaJ, yedQ and
CC       yegE (PubMed:18765794). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18765794,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19332833, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20303158,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:20346719}.
