CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in lipopolysaccharide (LPS) biosynthesis.
CC       Catalyzes the transfer of two 3-deoxy-D-manno-octulosonate (Kdo)
CC       residues from CMP-Kdo to lipid IV(A), the tetraacyldisaccharide-
CC       1,4'-bisphosphate precursor of lipid A.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10951204, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Lipid IV(A) + CMP-beta-Kdo = alpha-Kdo-(2->6)-
CC       lipid IV(A) + CMP. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10951204,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Alpha-Kdo-(2->6)-lipid IV(A) + CMP-beta-Kdo =
CC       alpha-Kdo-(2->4)-alpha-Kdo-(2->6)-lipid IV(A) + CMP.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10951204, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Catalytic activity is inhibited by the
CC       antibiotic polymixin B and by Re endotoxin.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=52 uM for lipid IV(A) (at pH 8) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828};
CC         KM=88 uM for CMP-Kdo (at pH 8) {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828};
CC         Vmax=18 umol/min/mg enzyme (at pH 8)
CC         {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828};
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 7. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Glycolipid biosynthesis; KDO(2)-lipid A biosynthesis;
CC       KDO(2)-lipid A from CMP-3-deoxy-D-manno-octulosonate and lipid
CC       IV(A): step 1/4. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Glycolipid biosynthesis; KDO(2)-lipid A biosynthesis;
CC       KDO(2)-lipid A from CMP-3-deoxy-D-manno-octulosonate and lipid
CC       IV(A): step 2/4. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828}.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Bacterial outer membrane biogenesis; LPS core
CC       biosynthesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1577828}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:1577828}; Single-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:1577828}; Cytoplasmic side
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:1577828}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal half of KdtA is responsible for determining
CC       the number of Kdo residues that are transferred to lipid IVA.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:20394418}.
CC   -!- PTM: Degraded by the protease FtsH; therefore FtsH regulates the
CC       addition of the sugar moiety to the LPS and thus the maturation of
CC       the LPS precursor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18776015}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene display growth
CC       defects, absence of Kdo transferase activity, and accumulate
CC       massive amounts of lipid IV(A). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7499229}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the glycosyltransferase group 1 family.
CC       Glycosyltransferase 30 subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
