CC   -!- FUNCTION: Reversibly transfers an adenylyl group from ATP to 4'-
CC       phosphopantetheine, yielding dephospho-CoA (dPCoA) and
CC       pyrophosphate (PubMed:10480925). The crystal structures in complex
CC       with substrates suggest the enyzme stabilizes the transition state
CC       without the direct involvement of enzymatic functional groups in
CC       the reaction (PubMed:11812124). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10480925,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11812124}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + pantetheine 4'-phosphate = diphosphate +
CC       3'-dephospho-CoA. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00151,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10480925, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17873050}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mg(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:18420;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000269|PubMed:11812124};
CC       Note=Crystallized in the absence of Mg(2+), the catalytic metal is
CC       not bound by the protein but probably by non-esterified oxygen
CC       atoms from ATP and/or ordered H(2)O (PubMed:11812124).
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:11812124};
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Inhibited by CoA, which competes with ATP, 4'-
CC       phosphopantetheine and 3'-dephospho-CoA (PubMed:12837781). Binds
CC       0.5 CoA tightly per monomer in the same position as 3'-dephospho-
CC       CoA but in a different fashion (PubMed:12837781, PubMed:17873050).
CC       CoA is not a substrate for the enzyme (PubMed:10480925).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12837781, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17873050,
CC       ECO:0000305|PubMed:10480925}.

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:10480925, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11812124,
