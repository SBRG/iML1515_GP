CC   -!- FUNCTION: In eubacteria ppGpp (guanosine 3'-diphosphate 5-'
CC       diphosphate) is a mediator of the stringent response that
CC       coordinates a variety of cellular activities in response to
CC       changes in nutritional abundance. This enzyme catalyzes both the
CC       synthesis and degradation of ppGpp. The second messengers ppGpp
CC       and c-di-GMP together control biofilm formation in response to
CC       translational stress; ppGpp represses biofilm formation while c-
CC       di-GMP induces it. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14622409,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19460094}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + GTP = AMP + guanosine 3'-diphosphate 5'-
CC       triphosphate.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Guanosine 3',5'-bis(diphosphate) + H(2)O =
CC       guanosine 5'-diphosphate + diphosphate.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=Mn(2+); Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:29035;
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Purine metabolism; ppGpp biosynthesis; ppGpp from GDP:
CC       step 1/1.
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Purine metabolism; ppGpp biosynthesis; ppGpp from GTP:
CC       step 1/2.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with ObgE/CgtA (AC P42641); this association is
CC       not required for pre-50S ribosome binding. Under both amino acid
CC       and carbon starvation conditions about half of the protein
CC       dissociates from the ribosome. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15292126}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P0A6A8:acpP; NbExp=5; IntAct=EBI-543228, EBI-542566;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17616600}.
CC       Note=Is associated with pre-50S ribosomal subunits in a salt-
CC       dependent manner.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: In the presence of wild-type relA, spoT
CC       cannot be deleted because ppGpp accumulation is toxic. In the
CC       double relA/spoT deletion (a ppGpp0 mutant) there is a very large
CC       increase in biofilm formation (in a csrA-disrupted background)
CC       (PubMed:19460094). The double relA/spoT deletion obviates
CC       persister cell formation in a hipA7 mutant (PubMed:14622409,
CC       PubMed:26051177). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14622409,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:19460094, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26051177}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the RelA/SpoT family. {ECO:0000305}.
