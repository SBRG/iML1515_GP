CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the formation of the second messenger cAMP
CC       from ATP. Its transcript is probably degraded by endoribonuclease
CC       LS (rnlA), decreasing cAMP levels and the negative regulator Crp-
CC       cAMP, which then induces its own transcription again.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP = 3',5'-cyclic AMP + diphosphate.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: The regulatory domain is involved in the
CC       regulation of cyclase activity by the carbon source. Activated by
CC       the PTS system, glucose-specific IIA component (CRR).
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P15038:helD; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-1116685, EBI-551473;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Repressed by the Crp-cAMP complex. Expression increases
CC       during growth, decreasing again in stationary phase; more strongly
CC       induced in an rnlA deletion mutant, levels remain high even in
CC       stationary phase (at protein level). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19019153,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2982847}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Not essential, eliminates the NaCl
CC       sensitivity of an rnlA deletion mutant.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19019153}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the adenylyl cyclase class-1 family.
CC       {ECO:0000305}.
