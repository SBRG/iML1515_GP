CC       D-ribitylamino)uracil, and thus could be involved in the
CC       riboflavin biosynthesis pathway (PubMed:24123841). Is also able to
CC       dephosphorylate flavin mononucleotide (FMN) and other phosphoric
CC       acid esters (PubMed:16990279) (PubMed:24123841). YigB is important
CC       for the formation of dormant persister cells (PubMed:18519731).
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16990279, ECO:0000269|PubMed:18519731,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:24123841}.
