CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the final step of fatty acid oxidation in
CC       which acetyl-CoA is released and the CoA ester of a fatty acid two
CC       carbons shorter is formed. Involved in the aerobic and anaerobic
CC       degradation of long-chain fatty acids.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12535077, ECO:0000269|PubMed:368024}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acyl-CoA + acetyl-CoA = CoA + 3-oxoacyl-CoA.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=96 uM for acetoacetyl-CoA {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8454629};
CC         KM=102 uM for CoASH {ECO:0000269|PubMed:8454629};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Lipid metabolism; fatty acid beta-oxidation.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Heterotetramer of two alpha chains (FadB) and two beta
CC       chains (FadA). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:368024}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Repressed by FadR in the absence of LCFAs (fatty acids
CC       of, at least, 12 carbon atoms). When LCFAs are present in the
CC       medium, they are converted to long-chain acyl-CoAs which bind to
CC       FadR resulting in its release from the DNA and thus derepression
CC       of the transcription.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the thiolase family. {ECO:0000305}.
