CC   -!- FUNCTION: Transfers a GMP moiety from GTP to Mo-molybdopterin (Mo-
CC       MPT) cofactor (Moco or molybdenum cofactor) to form Mo-
CC       molybdopterin guanine dinucleotide (Mo-MGD) cofactor. Is also
CC       involved in the biosynthesis of the bis-MGD form of the Moco
CC       cofactor (Mo-bisMGD) in which the metal is symmetrically ligated
CC       by the dithiolene groups of two MGD molecules. Is necessary and
CC       sufficient for the in vitro activation of the DMSOR molybdoenzyme
CC       that uses the Mo-bisMGD form of molybdenum cofactor, which implies
CC       formation and efficient insertion of the cofactor into the enzyme
CC       without the need of a chaperone. Is specific for GTP since other
CC       nucleotides such as ATP and GMP cannot be utilized.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10978348, ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648082,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21081498, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8020507}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: GTP + molybdenum cofactor = diphosphate +
CC       guanylyl molybdenum cofactor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10978348,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21081498}.

CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:8020507}.

CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Is expressed at very low levels under both aerobic and
CC       anaerobic growth conditions. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:7551035}.
CC   -!- DOMAIN: The N-terminal domain determines nucleotide recognition
CC       and specific binding, while the C-terminal domain determines the
CC       specific binding to the target protein. When the N-terminal domain
CC       of MobA is fused to the C-terminal domain of MocA, comparable
CC       kinetic constants as wild-type MobA are obtained with GTP, and the
CC       activity with CTP is completely lost. Consistent results are
CC       obtained when the N-terminal domain of MocA is fused to the C-
CC       terminal domain of MobA: the kinetic constants with CTP are
CC       comparable with the ones found for wild-type MocA, although no
CC       activity with GTP is detected. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21081498}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene are chlorate-
CC       resistant, fail to synthesize MGD and accumulate elevated
CC       quantities of MPT. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1648082}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the MobA family. {ECO:0000305}.
