CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the catabolism of L-rhamnose (6-deoxy-L-
CC       mannose). It could also play a role in the metabolism of some rare
CC       sugars such as L-fructose. Catalyzes the transfer of the gamma-
CC       phosphate group from ATP to the 1-hydroxyl group of L-rhamnulose
CC       to yield L-rhamnulose 1-phosphate. Uridine triphosphate (UTP),
CC       cytidine 5-triphosphate (CTP), guanosine 5-triphosphate (GTP), and
CC       thymidine triphosphate (TTP) also can act as phosphoryl donors. It
CC       can also phosphorylate L-fuculose and L-xylulose.
CC       {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01535, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14264882,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:16674975, ECO:0000269|PubMed:5341476}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + L-rhamnulose = ADP + L-rhamnulose 1-
CC       phosphate. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01535,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14264882, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16674975}.
