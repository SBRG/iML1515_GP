CC   -!- FUNCTION: Catalyzes the reversible formation of fructose 6-
CC       phosphate from dihydroxyacetone and D-glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate
CC       via an aldolization reaction. Can utilize hydroxyacetone as an
CC       alternative donor substrate. Is also able to catalyze the direct
CC       self-aldol addition of glycolaldehyde. Is less catalytically
CC       efficient than the isozyme FsaA. Does not display transaldolase
CC       activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11120740, ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: D-fructose 6-phosphate = glycerone + D-
CC       glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:11120740,
CC       ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=27 mM for dihydroxyacetone {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC         KM=15 mM for hydroxyacetone {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC         KM=0.28 mM for glycolaldehyde (as donor substrate in the self-
CC         aldol addition of glycolaldehyde) {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC         KM=65 mM for glycolaldehyde (as acceptor substrate in the self-
CC         aldol addition of glycolaldehyde) {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC         KM=0.65 mM for D,L-glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate
CC         {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC         KM=6.70 mM for D-fructose 6-phosphate {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC         KM=210 mM for D-fructose {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC         KM=271 mM for D-sorbose {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC         Note=Catalytic efficiency is 28-fold higher using glyceraldehyde
CC         3-phosphate as acceptor substrate and hydroxyacetone as donor
CC         substrate than with dihydroxyacetone as the donor.;
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 8.5 for the retroaldol activity on F6P.
CC         {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC       Temperature dependence:
CC         Is not thermally stable at temperatures higher than 60 degrees
CC         Celsius. Is less thermostable than the isozyme FsaA.
CC         {ECO:0000269|Ref.6};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodecamer. {ECO:0000269|Ref.6}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cytoplasm {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the transaldolase family. Type 3A
CC       subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
