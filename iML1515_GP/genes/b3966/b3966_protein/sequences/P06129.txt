CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the active translocation of vitamin B12
CC       (cyanocobalamin) across the outer membrane to the periplasmic
CC       space. It derives its energy for transport by interacting with the
CC       trans-periplasmic membrane protein TonB. Is also a receptor for
CC       bacteriophages BF23 and C1, and for A and E colicins.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10485884, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2687240,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2982793}.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Calcium increases vitamin B12 binding affinity
CC       by a factor of 50-100. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14499604}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Interacts with TonB. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10485884,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14528295}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell outer membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12595710, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12652322,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2687240, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2982793}; Multi-
CC       pass membrane protein {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12595710,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12652322, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2687240,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:2982793}.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Constitutively expressed. Primary control of btuB
CC       expression by cobalamin occurs at the level of translation
CC       initiation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:9852020}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the TonB-dependent receptor family. BtuB
CC       (TC 1.B.14.3.1) subfamily. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01531,
CC       ECO:0000305}.
