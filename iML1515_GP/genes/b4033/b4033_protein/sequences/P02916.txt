CC   -!- FUNCTION: Part of the binding-protein-dependent transport system
CC       for maltose; probably responsible for the translocation of the
CC       substrate across the membrane.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: The complex is composed of two ATP-binding proteins
CC       (MalK), two transmembrane proteins (MalG and MalF) and a solute-
CC       binding protein (MalE). Protein stability and stable complex
CC       formation require YidC. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10809785,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18456666}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       P0AEX9:malE; NbExp=2; IntAct=EBI-1118919, EBI-369910;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell inner membrane
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18456666}; Multi-pass membrane protein
CC       {ECO:0000255|PROSITE-ProRule:PRU00441,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:18456666}; Periplasmic side
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18456666}. Note=A substantial portion of it
CC       protrudes into the periplasmic space; inserts in an SRP- and Sec-
CC       dependent, YidC-independent fashion into the membrane.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: When MalF EAA loop mutations are made concomitantly
CC       with MalG EAA loop mutations, a complete loss of transport and
CC       complex formation is observed, except for the Gly-407. This
CC       suggests that the MalF-MalG interaction may be important for the
CC       proper assembly and also for the correct function of the
CC       transporter.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the binding-protein-dependent transport
CC       system permease family. MalFG subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
