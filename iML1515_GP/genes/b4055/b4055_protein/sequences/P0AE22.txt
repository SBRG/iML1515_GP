CC   -!- FUNCTION: Dephosphorylates several organic phosphate monoesters
CC       including 3'- and 5'-nucleotides, 2'-deoxy-5'-nucleotides, pNPP,
CC       phenyl phosphate, glycerol 2-phosphate, ribose 5-phosphate, O-
CC       phospho-L-amino acids and phytic acid, showing the highest
CC       activity with aryl phosphoesters (pNPP, phenyl phosphate and O-
CC       phospho-L-tyrosine), and to a lesser extent with 3'- and 5'-
CC       nucleotides. No activity toward ATP, phosphodiesters, glycerol-1-
CC       phosphate, glucose 1-phosphate, glucose 6-phosphate, NADP, GTP or
CC       3',5'-cAMP, ADP or ATP. Also has a phosphotransferase activity
CC       catalyzing the transfer of low-energy phosphate groups from
CC       organic phosphate monoesters to free hydroxyl groups of various
CC       organic compounds. Capable of transferring phosphate from either
CC       pNPP or UMP to adenosine or uridine. Does not exhibit nucleotide
CC       phosphomutase activity. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16297670,
