CC   -!- FUNCTION: Aminoalkylphosphonate N-acetyltransferase which is able
CC       to acetylate a range of aminoalkylphosphonic acids, including
CC       aminomethylphosphonate, (S)-1-aminoethylphosphonate and 2-
CC       aminoethyl- and 3-aminopropylphosphonate, using acetyl-CoA as
CC       acetyl donor. Is required for the utilization of
CC       aminomethylphosphonate and (S)-1-aminoethylphosphonate as a
CC       phosphate source via the C-P lyase pathway. Is also essential for
CC       the detoxification of (S)-1-aminoethylphosphonate, a structural
CC       analog of D-alanine that has bacteriocidal properties due to
CC       inhibition of cell wall synthesis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23056305}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acetyl-CoA + aminomethylphosphonate = CoA + N-
CC       acetamidomethylphosphonate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23056305}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Acetyl-CoA + (S)-1-aminoethylphosphonate = CoA
CC       + N-acetyl-(S)-1-aminoethylphosphonate.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23056305}.
CC   -!- COFACTOR:
CC       Name=a divalent metal cation; Xref=ChEBI:CHEBI:60240;
CC         Evidence={ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8ZKE6};
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Homodimer. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8ZKE6}.
CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Cells lacking this gene have lost the
CC       ability to utilize aminomethylphosphonate or (S)-1-
CC       aminoethylphosphonate as the sole source of phosphate for growth,
CC       but are still able to utilize the other aminoalkylphosphonates 2-
CC       aminoethyl- and 3-aminopropylphosphonate, and alkylphosphonates.
CC       They are also much more susceptible to the bacteriocidal effect of
CC       (S)-1-aminoethylphosphonate, even with phosphate or D-alanine
CC       present. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:23056305}.
