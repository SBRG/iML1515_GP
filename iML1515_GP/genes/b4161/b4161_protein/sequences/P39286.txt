CC   -!- FUNCTION: One of at least 4 proteins (Era, RbfA, RimM and
CC       RsgA/YjeQ) that assist in the late maturation steps of the
CC       functional core of the 30S ribosomal subunit (PubMed:18223068,
CC       PubMed:21102555, PubMed:21303937, PubMed:25904134,
CC       PubMed:27382067). Binds the 30S subunit contacting the head,
CC       platform, and rRNA helix 44, which may assist the last maturation
CC       stages (PubMed:21788480, PubMed:21960487). Removes RbfA from
CC       mature, but not immature 30S ribosomes in a GTP-dependent manner;
CC       95% removal in the presence of GTP, 90% removal in GMP-PNP and 65%
CC       removal in the presence of GDP (PubMed:21102555, PubMed:25904134).
CC       Circulary permuted GTPase that catalyzes rapid hydrolysis of GTP
CC       with a slow catalytic turnover (PubMed:12220175). Dispensible for
CC       viability, but important for overall fitness. The intrinsic GTPase
CC       activity is stimulated by the presence of 30S (160-fold increase
CC       in kcat) or 70S (96-fold increase in kcat) ribosomes
CC       (PubMed:14973029). Mature 30S ribosomes stimulate intrinsic GTPase
CC       more than do immature 30S ribosomes (PubMed:25904134). Ribosome-
CC       associated GTPase activity is stimulated by RbfA
CC       (PubMed:21102555). The GTPase is inhibited by aminoglycoside
CC       antibiotics such as neomycin and paromycin (PubMed:15466596)
CC       streptomycin and spectinomycin (PubMed:15828870). This inhibition
CC       is not due to competition for binding sites on the 30S or 70S
CC       ribosome (PubMed:15828870). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12220175,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14973029, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15466596,

CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer (Probable). All of the protein is associated with
CC       ribosomes; the ratio is substoichiometric at 1:200 RsgA/ribosome
CC       (PubMed:14973029). Association is tightest with the 30S subunit in
CC       the presence of the non-hydrolyzable GTP analog GMP-PNP
CC       (PubMed:14973029, PubMed:15466596). 2 cryoelectron microscopy
CC       (cryo-EM) structures in complex with 30S ribosomes have been
CC       resolved; the protein is determined to bind to different but
CC       partially overlapping regions of the 30S ribosomal subunit
CC       (PubMed:21788480, PubMed:21960487). One cryo-EM study suggests it
CC       contacts ribosomal proteins S3, S12 and S13 as well as 16S rRNA
CC       (PubMed:21788480). Another cryo-EM study shows it to bind 16S
CC       rRNA, no ribosomal proteins, and to cover the sites of
CC       intersubunit bridges B2a, B3 and B7a (PubMed:21960487). Has a
CC       significant preference for mature versus immature 30S ribosomes in
CC       the presence of GMP-PNP (PubMed:21102555, PubMed:27382067).
CC       Another study shows it binds equally well to mature and immature
CC       30S ribosomal subunits in the presence of GMP-PNP
CC       (PubMed:25904134). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14973029,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:15466596, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21102555,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21788480, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21960487,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25904134, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27382067,

CC   -!- DOMAIN: Has 3 domains; an N-terminal OB-like domain (about
CC       residues 1-100), a central, circularly permutated GTPase module
CC       (residues 104-270) and the C-terminal zinc-finger domain (residues
CC       280 to 350) (PubMed:25904134). The C-terminal domain has 2
CC       regions; the zinc-binding domain (residues 287-319) is required
CC       for binding to 30S ribosomes while the extreme C-terminus
CC       (residues 320-350) helps remove RbfA from the mature 30S subunit.
CC       {ECO:0000305|PubMed:25904134}.

CC   -!- DISRUPTION PHENOTYPE: Reduced growth rate, reduced 70S ribosomes,
CC       accumulation of 17S rRNA (the precursor of 16S rRNA)
CC       (PubMed:15466596, PubMed:21303937, PubMed:25904134,
CC       PubMed:27382067). Ribosomal proteins S2 and S21 not found in 30S
CC       subunits, decreased S3 and RbfA in 30S subunits, distortion of
CC       rRNA helix 44 near the decoding center (PubMed:21303937,
CC       PubMed:27382067). The phenotype is partially suppressed by
CC       overexpression of a number of genes involved in ribosome function,
CC       including infB, era and ksgA (PubMed:15466596). Double rbfA-rsgA
CC       deletion mutants have the same phenotype as single mutants
CC       (PubMed:21102555). Single rgsA deletion is suppressed by a number
CC       of mutants in RbfA but not by wild-type RbfA; in all the RbfA
CC       mutants less RbfA is found bound to the 30S ribosome
CC       (PubMed:21102555). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15466596,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:21102555, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21303937,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:25904134, ECO:0000269|PubMed:27382067}.
