CC   -!- FUNCTION: Outer membrane channel protein allowing the entry of N-
CC       acetylneuraminic acid (Neu5Ac, the most abundant sialic acid on
CC       host cell surfaces) into the bacteria (Probable). NanC proteins
CC       form high-conductance channels which are open at low membrane
CC       potentials and which have a weak anion selectivity.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15743943, ECO:0000305}.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Monomer. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19796645}.
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Cell outer membrane.
CC   -!- INDUCTION: Induced by N-acetylneuraminate and modulated by N-
CC       acetylglucosamine. This regulation occurs via the NanR and NagC
CC       regulators. NanC expression is also activated by the regulators
CC       cyclic AMP-catabolite activator protein, OmpR, and CpxR.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15743943}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the oligogalacturonate-specific porin KdgM
CC       (TC 1.B.35) family. NanC subfamily. {ECO:0000305}.
