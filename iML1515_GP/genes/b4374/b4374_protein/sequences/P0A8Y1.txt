CC   -!- FUNCTION: Nucleotidase that shows high phosphatase activity toward
CC       non-canonical pyrimidine nucleotides and three canonical
CC       nucleoside 5'-monophosphates (UMP, dUMP, and dTMP), and very low
CC       activity against TDP, IMP, UDP, GMP, dGMP, AMP, dAMP, and 6-
CC       phosphogluconate. Appears to function as a house-cleaning
CC       nucleotidase in vivo, since the general nucleotidase activity of
CC       YjjG allows it to protect cells against non-canonical pyrimidine
CC       derivatives such as 5-fluoro-2'-deoxyuridine, 5-fluorouridine, 5-
CC       fluoroorotate, 5-fluorouracil, and 5-aza-2'-deoxycytidine, and
CC       prevents the incorporation of potentially mutagenic nucleotides
CC       into DNA. Its dUMP phosphatase activity that catalyzes the
CC       hydrolysis of dUMP to deoxyuridine is necessary for thymine
CC       utilization via the thymine salvage pathway. Is strictly specific
CC       to substrates with 5'-phosphates and shows no activity against
CC       nucleoside 2'- or 3'-monophosphates. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15489502,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17189366, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17286574}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: A 5'-ribonucleotide + H(2)O = a ribonucleoside
CC       + phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15489502,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:17286574}.
