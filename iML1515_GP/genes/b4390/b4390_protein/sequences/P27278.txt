CC   -!- FUNCTION: This enzyme has three activities: DNA binding,
CC       nicotinamide mononucleotide (NMN) adenylyltransferase and
CC       ribosylnicotinamide (RN) kinase. The DNA-binding domain binds to
CC       the nadB operator sequence in an NAD- and ATP-dependent manner. As
CC       NAD levels increase within the cell, the affinity of NadR for the
CC       nadB operator regions of nadA, nadB, and pncB increases,
CC       repressing the transcription of these genes. The RN kinase
CC       activity catalyzes the phosphorylation of RN to form nicotinamide
CC       ribonucleotide. The NMN adenylyltransferase activity catalyzes the
CC       transfer of the AMP moiety of ATP to nicotinamide ribonucleotide
CC       to form NAD(+). The NMN adenylyltransferase domain also functions
CC       as the NAD and ATP sensor. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10464228}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + nicotinamide ribonucleotide =
CC       diphosphate + NAD(+).
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + 1-(beta-D-ribofuranosyl)-nicotinamide =
CC       ADP + beta-nicotinamide D-ribonucleotide.
CC   -!- ENZYME REGULATION: Feed-back regulated by NAD. A high level of NAD
CC       causes NadR to lose enzymatic activity and repress several NAD
CC       synthetic genes; conversely, a low NAD level activates the
CC       assimilatory enzymatic activities and leads to derepression of
CC       biosynthetic genes (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- BIOPHYSICOCHEMICAL PROPERTIES:
CC       Kinetic parameters:
CC         KM=0.7 mM for NMN {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10464228};
CC         KM=1.7 uM for ATP {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10464228};
CC       pH dependence:
CC         Optimum pH is 8.6. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10464228};
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cofactor biosynthesis; NAD(+) biosynthesis [regulation].
CC   -!- PATHWAY: Cofactor biosynthesis; NAD(+) biosynthesis; NAD(+) from
