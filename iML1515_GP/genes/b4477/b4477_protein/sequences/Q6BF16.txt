CC   -!- FUNCTION: Involved in the degradation of galactose via the DeLey-
CC       Doudoroff pathway. Catalyzes the reversible, stereospecific retro-
CC       aldol cleavage of 2-keto-3-deoxy-6-phosphogalactonate (KDPGal) to
CC       pyruvate and D-glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate. In the synthetic
CC       direction, it catalyzes the addition of pyruvate to electrophilic
CC       aldehydes with re-facial selectivity. It can use a limited number
CC       of aldehyde substrates, including D-glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate
CC       (natural substrate), D-glyceraldehyde, glycolaldehyde, 2-
CC       pyridinecarboxaldehyde, D-ribose, D-erythrose and D-threose. It
CC       efficiently catalyzes aldol addition only using pyruvate as the
CC       nucleophilic component and accepts both stereochemical
CC       configurations at C2 of the electrophile.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:17981470, ECO:0000269|PubMed:324806}.
